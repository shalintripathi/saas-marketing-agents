# CATALYST Quickstart: Implementing Your First Campaign

## Before You Begin: Prerequisites

### 1. Technology Prerequisites
You need these systems in place (or committed to setup during Phase 2):
- **CRM System**: HubSpot, Salesforce, or Pipedrive (for lead tracking and pipeline visibility)
- **Email Platform**: Marketo, HubSpot, or Klaviyo (for email marketing)
- **Analytics**: Google Analytics 4 or equivalent (for traffic and conversion tracking)
- **Project Management**: Linear, Asana, or Monday (for coordination)
- **Cloud Storage**: Google Drive, Notion, or Sharepoint (for asset repository)

### 2. Human Resources
Minimum viable team by operating mode:

**CATALYST-Full** (59 agents):
- Option A: Full 15-25 person in-house marketing team
- Option B: 5-8 person core team + agency partners filling specialist roles
- Option C: Network of freelance specialists coordinated by 2-3 project managers

**CATALYST-Sprint** (15-25 agents):
- Option A: 8-12 person team
- Option B: 4-6 person core team + 3-4 contractors
- Option C: Freelance specialist network for specific campaign

**CATALYST-Micro** (5-10 agents):
- Option A: 2-3 person team
- Option B: 1-2 people + freelancers for specialized work
- Option C: Single CATALYST coordinator + specialists as-needed

### 3. Time Commitment
- **CATALYST-Full**: 12-16 weeks of active coordination
- **CATALYST-Sprint**: 2-6 weeks of active coordination
- **CATALYST-Micro**: 1-5 days of active coordination

At minimum, designate a CATALYST Coordinator (10-20 hours/week depending on mode) who manages phases, runs meetings, and ensures handoffs happen.

## Step 1: Choose Your Operating Mode (15 minutes)

### Quick Decision Framework

| Mode | Choose If | Timeline | Agents | Examples |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|----------|
| **CATALYST-Full** | You're building annual strategy OR you're new to B2B SaaS marketing OR you need comprehensive positioning | 12-16 weeks | 40-59 | New GTM for startup; major product launch; market expansion; annual strategy refresh |
| **CATALYST-Sprint** | You have strategy but need to execute campaigns OR you're fixing underperforming channels OR you're running quarterly initiatives | 2-6 weeks | 15-25 | Product feature launch; response to competition; seasonal campaign; lead generation blitz |
| **CATALYST-Micro** | You need one specific asset fast OR you want to test CATALYST with a small project OR you have urgent opportunity | 1-5 days | 5-10 | Landing page creation; ad creative production; competitive analysis brief; email sequence; content piece |

**Decision Tool**: If you have strategy and execution infrastructure (positioning defined, channels running, analytics configured), pick **CATALYST-Sprint**. If you're starting from scratch, pick **CATALYST-Full**. If you just need one thing fast, pick **CATALYST-Micro**.

## Step 2: Define Success Metrics (30 minutes)

You need clarity on what success looks like before you start. Create a simple 1-page document:

### Template: Success Definition

```
PROJECT: [Campaign or strategy name]
OPERATING MODE: [Full/Sprint/Micro]

PRIMARY SUCCESS METRIC (The number that matters most):
- Metric: [e.g., "Marketing-qualified leads"]
- Current State: [e.g., "50 MQLs/month"]
- Target: [e.g., "120 MQLs/month"]
- Timeline: [e.g., "By end of Q2 2026"]

SECONDARY SUCCESS METRICS (Supporting metrics):
1. [e.g., "Cost per MQL: Currently $300, target $180"]
2. [e.g., "Email open rate: Currently 18%, target 24%"]
3. [e.g., "Website traffic: Currently 8K visits/month, target 15K visits/month"]

BUSINESS CONTEXT:
- Why does this matter right now? [e.g., "Series B funding deadline; need to show traction"]
- What would failure look like? [e.g., "Less than 75 MQLs/month"]
- Budget availability: [e.g., "$50K for paid advertising"]

CONSTRAINTS:
- Timeline constraints? [e.g., "Must launch by March 15"]
- Resource constraints? [e.g., "2 designers available; 1 copywriter"]
- Technical constraints? [e.g., "Can't modify current website until April"]

STAKEHOLDERS & APPROVAL:
- Primary stakeholder: [Name, Title]
- Budget authority: [Name, Title]
- Escalation path for decisions: [Names]
```

**Why this matters**: Unclear success metrics cause misalignment. This document prevents "I thought we were optimizing for lead quality but you wanted quantity" arguments.

## Step 5: Run Your First Phase (2-4 weeks for Full, varies for Sprint/Micro)

### What Happens in Each Phase

#### Phase 0: Discovery & Audit (2-3 weeks, CATALYST-Full)
**Team**: Customer Insights Researcher, Competitive Intelligence Analyst, SEO Auditor, Analytics Analyst, Persona Builder
**Deliverables**:
- Customer research findings (personas, pain points, buying process)
- Competitive analysis (3-5 competitors, positioning map)
- SEO audit and opportunities
- Market sizing and TAM analysis

**Success Gate**: Stakeholder approval of key findings; at least 3 actionable insights identified

#### Phase 1: Strategy & Planning (1-2 weeks, CATALYST-Full)
**Team**: Positioning Strategist, Messaging Architect, Campaign Coordinator, Budget Optimizer
**Deliverables**:
- Positioning statement (how you're different)
- Message house (value prop, key messages, proof points)
- Campaign architecture (3-4 major campaigns)
- Budget allocation and timeline

**Success Gate**: Executive approval of positioning and messaging; budgets signed off

#### Phase 2: Foundation & Infrastructure (1-2 weeks, CATALYST-Full)
**Team**: Marketing Ops Architect, CRM Specialist, Email Deliverability, Automation Engineer
**Deliverables**:
- CRM configured and tested
- Email infrastructure set up and verified
- Analytics tracking implemented
- Brand guidelines and design system

**Success Gate**: All systems operational; tracking verified; 100% email deliverability confirmed

#### Phase 3: Build (2-4 weeks, CATALYST-Full)
**Team**: Content specialist, Copywriter, Video producer, Ad creative producer, Landing page specialist
**Deliverables**:
- Blog content (4+ pieces)
- Landing pages (3+)
- Ad creative (multiple variations per channel)
- Email sequences
- Sales enablement assets

**Success Gate**: All assets created, QA'd, scheduled

#### Phase 4: Launch (1 week, CATALYST-Full)
**Team**: Launch Manager, Paid Ads Specialist, Email specialist, Social Lead, Outbound Specialist
**Deliverables**:
- Live campaigns across all channels
- Verified tracking and baseline metrics
- Daily monitoring and initial optimizations

**Success Gate**: All channels live; data flowing correctly

#### Phase 5: Optimize (Ongoing, typically 4+ weeks for CATALYST-Full)
**Team**: CRO Specialist, Performance Analyst, Data Storyteller, Attribution Analyst
**Deliverables**:
- A/B tests running (3-5 concurrent tests)
- Weekly optimization recommendations
- Monthly performance reporting
- Learning documentation

**Success Gate**: Week-over-week performance improvement; optimization roadmap defined

### Daily Operating Rhythm

**CATALYST-Full Daily Standup** (15 minutes, 9:00am)

## Step 6: Navigate Phase Transitions (Quality Gates)

At the end of each phase, you hit a quality gate. This is a 60-minute review meeting:

### Quality Gate Process

**Before the Meeting** (Phase Lead completes, 2 hours before meeting):
- [ ] Document all deliverables and where they live (links, files)
- [ ] Create one-page summary of key findings/decisions
- [ ] List any issues or concerns discovered
- [ ] Identify what worked well and what was difficult

**During the Meeting** (60 minutes):
- [ ] Phase Lead presents deliverables (10 min)
- [ ] Stakeholder Q&A (10 min)
- [ ] Review against quality gate criteria (15 min)
- [ ] Discuss any concerns or issues (15 min)
- [ ] Decision: Approve to advance, or require remediation (10 min)

**After the Meeting**:
- [ ] Document approval or remediation items
- [ ] Next phase lead prepares kickoff (if approved)
- [ ] Remediation team works on issues (typically 3-5 days)

### Common Quality Gate Blockers & Solutions

| Issue | Solution | Typical Timeline |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| Missing research | Prioritize urgent questions; skip nice-to-haves | 3-5 days |
| Unclear messaging | Run customer validation interviews | 1 week |
| Budget not approved | Present options; get executive decision | 2-3 days |
| Systems not ready | Run in manual/interim mode; parallel track fixes | Can proceed; fix in next phase |
| Insufficient assets | Reduce channels; focus on highest-ROI; parallel production | Can proceed with core channels |

**Typical 4-Week Timeline**:
- Day 1-2: Kickoff + strategy refinement
- Day 3-7: Rapid build phase (content, creative, pages)
- Day 8-10: Final QA and launch prep
- Day 11-28: Launch and optimization (daily monitoring, 2-3x weekly optimizations)

**Weekly Meetings**:
- Daily 10am: 15-minute standup
- MWF 3pm: 30-minute sync call
- Fridays 4pm: Weekly performance review

### For CATALYST-Micro (Specific Deliverable)

**Typical 3-5 Day Timeline**:
- Day 1: Kickoff + specifications
- Day 2-3: Build/creation

**Communication**:
- Kickoff call (15 min): Define exactly what you need

## Common Workflows

### Workflow 1: "We Have Strategy, Need Campaign Execution"
→ Use **CATALYST-Sprint**
- Start with Phase 1 (abbreviated, 2-3 days)
- Run full Phases 3-5
- Skip Phase 0-2 (assumes you have positioning, infrastructure)
- Typical timeline: 4 weeks
- Team: 15-20 agents focused on content and launch

### Workflow 2: "We Need a Specific Asset Fast"
→ Use **CATALYST-Micro**
- Define deliverable precisely (landing page? email sequence? ad creative?)
- Pull 5-8 specialists
- Work 1-5 days depending on complexity
- Typical timeline: 2-4 days
- Team: Hyper-focused specialists

### Workflow 3: "We're Building Marketing from Scratch"
→ Use **CATALYST-Full**
- Run all 6 phases
- Use CATALYST-Full to establish positioning, systems, and initial campaigns
- Typical timeline: 12-16 weeks
- Team: 40-59 agents across all specialties

### Workflow 4: "We Run Quarterly Campaigns"
→ Use **CATALYST-Sprint repeating**
- Q1: Run CATALYST-Full for annual positioning and setup
- Q2-Q4: Run CATALYST-Sprint for quarterly campaigns
- Typical timeline: 1 Full (16 weeks) + 3 Sprints (4 weeks each)
- Team: Core 8-10 + specialists as needed

## Troubleshooting: Common First-Project Issues

### "We're Getting Blocked on Decisions"
- **Root Cause**: Unclear decision authority or too many stakeholders needed for each call
- **Solution**: Create RACI matrix (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) before kickoff. Accountable person makes call after consulting stakeholders.
- **Prevention**: Assign clear decision authority for each phase before Phase 0 starts

### "Agents Aren't Clear on Handoffs"
- **Root Cause**: Handoff specifications aren't detailed enough or receiving agent doesn't understand context
- **Solution**: Use handoff template (see coordination/handoff-protocols.md) for every phase transition. Include context, acceptance criteria, and success definition.
- **Prevention**: Review first few handoffs carefully; coach agents on specification detail

### "We're Slipping the Timeline"
- **Root Cause**: Scope creeping or phase lead underestimating effort
- **Solution**: Stop new requests mid-phase. Triage incoming asks: Critical (do now), Valuable (add to next sprint), Nice-to-have (backlog).
- **Prevention**: Lock scope at phase start. Change control process for additions.

### "Quality isn't meeting expectations"
- **Root Cause**: Quality gates criteria too loose or not enforced
- **Solution**: Tighten gate criteria. Have subject matter expert review phase deliverables before gate meeting.
- **Prevention**: Define quality standards at phase kickoff. Use QA checklist from playbooks.

### "Team is burned out"
- **Root Cause**: Unrealistic timeline or unclear priorities
- **Solution**: Extend timeline or reduce scope. Pick 3-5 highest-impact initiatives; descope the rest.
- **Prevention**: Check team capacity/morale at weekly syncs. Scale team size to timeline.
