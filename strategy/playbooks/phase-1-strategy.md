# Phase 1: Strategy & Planning

**Duration**: 1-2 weeks for CATALYST-Full | 2-3 days for CATALYST-Sprint | Not typically used for CATALYST-Micro
**Agents Involved**: 7 strategy specialists (Positioning Strategist, Messaging Architect, Campaign Coordinator, Budget Optimizer, Goals Strategist, Audience Segmentation Specialist, Channel Strategy Planner)
**Output**: Strategic framework, messaging, campaign architecture, budgets, and timelines

## Overview

Phase 1 transforms Phase 0 discoveries into actionable strategy. This is where you make critical choices about positioning, messaging, campaign architecture, and resource allocation. These decisions cascade through all remaining phases, so Phase 1 is where strategic alignment across executives, marketing, and sales must be validated.

Phase 1 answers:
- What is our distinctive competitive position?
- What is our core value proposition and supporting message?
- How will we structure our marketing campaigns (2024 full-year view)?
- How should we allocate budget across channels and tactics?
- Who are our primary and secondary audiences?
- What are our success metrics and targets?

## Core Activities

### 1. Positioning Strategy Workshop (Positioning Strategist)

**Objective**: Define your clear, defensible competitive position.

**Execution**:

**Pre-Work** (Positioning Strategist, 8-10 hours):
- Review Phase 0 findings: Competitive analysis, customer insights, market opportunities
- Draft 3-4 positioning hypotheses:
  - Option A: Industry specialization (e.g., "The only solution built for mid-market SaaS")
  - Option B: Approach differentiation (e.g., "The only solution that combines AI + human review")
  - Option C: Outcome differentiation (e.g., "Fastest time-to-ROI: 30 days to measurable impact")
  - Option D: Feature/capability (e.g., "Only solution with native integrations for sales stack")

**Positioning Workshop** (2-3 hours, with CMO/VP Marketing, Product Lead, Sales Leader):
- Segment 1: Test each positioning hypothesis (1 hour)
  - For each hypothesis: Why could this be true? What would customers say? Where would we be vulnerable?
  - Eliminate weakest positioning
- Segment 2: Stress-test remaining positions (45 minutes)
  - Competitive response: How would competitors counter each position?
  - Customer validation: Which resonates most in Phase 0 research?
  - Capability validation: Can we authentically deliver on this promise?
- Segment 3: Finalize positioning statement (45 minutes)
  - Write single-sentence positioning (e.g., "We're the only [category] built specifically for [customer] to achieve [outcome]")
  - Define 3-4 proof points (why this is true; what validates it)
  - Identify key positioning dimensions (what makes this different)

**Positioning Framework** (document):
- Position statement (one sentence)
- Target customer (role, company, problem)
- Market opportunity (why now?)
- Competitive alternatives (who are we vs.?)
- Key differentiation (2-3 proof points)
- Brand promise (what customers can expect)
- Supporting pillars (3-4 areas of proof)

**Deliverable**:
- Positioning Statement Document (2 pages)
  - Rationale: Why this position? What did we learn from Phase 0 that points here?
  - Statement: Clear one-sentence positioning
  - Proof points: What validates this claim?
  - Competitive narrative: How do we position vs. alternatives?
  - Customer validation: Which Phase 0 interviews support this?

**Quality Criteria**:
- Positioning is differentiated (not generic "best, fastest, easiest")
- Validated in Phase 0 customer interviews (at least 2-3 customers mention resonance)
- Defensible against competitive responses
- Authentic (you can deliver on the promise)
- Test-ready (clear enough to validate in market)

### 2. Messaging Architecture (Messaging Architect)

**Objective**: Develop clear, consistent messaging across all customer touchpoints.

**Execution**:

**Message House Development** (20-25 hours):
A message house is organized like a house: foundation, pillars, roof. It ensures message consistency across all channels.

**Foundation (Core Value Proposition)**:
- The core benefit you deliver in 1-2 sentences
- Example: "Reduce customer churn by 30% with AI-powered retention strategies"
- Spans: Problem statement + your unique solution + quantified outcome

**Primary Pillars** (3 proof points; why your promise is true):

*Pillar 1*: [First proof point]
- Supporting evidence
- Customer example/data point
- Key message to support this pillar

*Pillar 2*: [Second proof point]
- Supporting evidence
- Customer example/data point
- Key message to support this pillar

*Pillar 3*: [Third proof point]
- Supporting evidence
- Customer example/data point
- Key message to support this pillar

**Secondary Pillars** (3-4 optional supporting messages):
- Ease of implementation
- Speed of ROI
- Integration capabilities
- Security/compliance
- Support quality

**Roof** (Desired perception/outcome):
- How customers should feel about your brand
- What belief you want to establish
- Example: "The trusted partner for customer success"

**Message Development Process** (40 hours total):

*Step 1: Hypothesis (4 hours)*
- Messaging Architect drafts initial message house based on positioning

*Step 2: Internal Alignment (4 hours)*
- Marketing, product, sales, leadership review and debate
- Revisions based on feedback
- Buy-in from all stakeholders

*Step 3: Customer Validation (12 hours)*
- 3-5 calls with customers testing messaging
- Questions: Does this resonate? What would you say differently?
- Synthesis: Which messages landed? Which fell flat?

*Step 4: Refinement (8 hours)*
- Messaging Architect integrates feedback
- Drafts final message house
- QA review for clarity, consistency, accuracy

*Step 5: Documentation (12 hours)*
- Create detailed messaging guidelines
- Message library organized by audience (CMO, prospect, customer, analyst)
- Proof points, customer quotes, case studies organized by message

**Audience-Specific Messaging** (optional, if major segment differences):

For each primary audience segment, develop variant messaging:
- **CMO/VP Marketing persona**: Focus on strategic impact, competitive advantage, reporting visibility
- **VP Sales persona**: Focus on deal efficiency, competitive wins, sales enablement
- **End-user persona**: Focus on daily ease-of-use, speed, integration
- **Prospect buyer persona**: Focus on ROI, risk mitigation, implementation timeline

**Deliverables**:
- Message House Document (3-4 pages): Visual + written with all components
- Messaging Guidelines (4-5 pages): How to apply messaging across channels
- Audience-Specific Messaging (2-3 pages per audience if variants needed)
- Quote Library: 10-15 customer quotes organized by message pillar

**Quality Criteria**:
- Core value prop is 1-2 clear sentences
- Each pillar has supporting evidence and customer validation
- Messaging tested with minimum 3-5 customers (documented feedback)
- Guidelines clear enough for agency/freelancer adoption
- Tone consistent across all message variants

### 3. Campaign Architecture & Planning (Campaign Coordinator)

**Objective**: Define the year's marketing campaigns and how they ladder to goals.

**Execution**:

**Campaign Planning** (16-20 hours):

*Step 1: Goal Definition (4 hours)*
- Review success metrics from QUICKSTART
- Define annual goal (e.g., "1,000 MQLs by year-end")
- Define quarterly goals (e.g., "250 MQLs Q1, 300 Q2, 350 Q3, 350 Q4")
- Define leading indicators (content published, ads impressions, emails sent)

*Step 2: Campaign Mapping (8 hours)*
- Identify 3-4 major campaigns that ladder to goals
- Campaign A: Q1 Launch campaign (awareness + lead gen)
- Campaign B: Q2 Seasonal campaign (if applicable)
- Campaign C: Q3 Thought leadership campaign
- Campaign D: Q4 Year-end customer campaign
- OR based on your business: Product launches, seasonal moments, customer expansion

For each campaign:
- Objective: What are we trying to achieve?
- Timeline: When does it run? What's the launch date?
- Primary audience: Who are we targeting?
- Channels: Which channels will we use? (Paid, organic, email, content, events)
- Budget: How much are we allocating?
- Success metrics: How do we know it worked?

*Step 3: Channel Strategy (4 hours)*
- For each channel (Paid Search, LinkedIn, Content, Email, Outbound, Social, Events):
  - Role in the funnel: Awareness? Consideration? Conversion?
  - Campaign contribution: Which campaigns will use this channel?
  - Investment level: Primary, secondary, or supporting?
  - Timing: When does activity ramp?

*Step 4: Creative & Asset Requirements (4 hours)*
- Map campaigns to content/creative needs
- What assets will Phase 3 need to produce?
- How many variations per asset?
- Timeline for asset production

**Annual Campaign Calendar** (visual):
- Timeline: Month-by-month view
- Campaign phases: When each campaign launches, runs, winds down
- Channel activation: When major channels activate
- Key dates: Product launches, holidays, events
- Resource allocation: Team focus by month

**Quarterly Campaign Plans** (2-3 pages per quarter):
- Campaign overview: Name, objective, timeline
- Channel strategy: Which channels? When? Expected performance
- Budget: How much spend per channel
- Team: Who's responsible? How many hours?
- Metrics: What are we measuring? What's success?

**Deliverables**:
- Annual Campaign Architecture (2-3 pages): 3-4 major campaigns, timeline, channels
- Quarterly Campaign Plans (8-12 pages): Detail for each quarter
- Channel Strategy Map (1-2 pages): Role of each channel, integration strategy
- Campaign Success Metrics (1-2 pages): KPIs and targets for each campaign

**Quality Criteria**:
- 3-4 major campaigns defined with clear objectives
- Quarterly detail sufficient for Phase 2 planning
- Channels clearly integrated (no siloes)
- Success metrics SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)

### 4. Budget Optimization & Allocation (Budget Optimizer)

**Objective**: Allocate budget across campaigns and channels to maximize ROI.

**Execution**:

**Budget Planning** (12-16 hours):

*Step 1: Total Budget Definition (2 hours)*
- Confirm total annual marketing budget (or quarterly, if working with CATALYST-Sprint)
- Understand constraints (fixed costs, must-haves, nice-to-haves)

*Step 2: Baseline Analysis (4 hours)*
- Review Phase 0 audit: Historical spend and ROI by channel
- Calculate: Cost per lead, customer acquisition cost, lifetime value by channel
- Identify: Underperforming channels (ROI < desired threshold)
- Identify: Overperforming channels (ROI > expected, room to grow)

*Step 3: Budget Allocation (6 hours)*
- Allocate budget across campaigns
  - Campaign A: 30% of budget (primary growth driver)
  - Campaign B: 25% of budget (secondary growth driver)
  - Campaign C: 20% of budget (retention/thought leadership)
  - Campaign D: 15% of budget (cross-sell/seasonal)
  - Holdback: 10% for testing/optimization

- Allocate budget across channels
  - Paid Search: If high ROI historically, maintain/grow
  - LinkedIn Ads: If targeting B2B, allocate accordingly
  - Content/SEO: Longer payoff, but compounding
  - Email: Highest ROI in most SaaS businesses (allocate accordingly)
  - Outbound: If relevant to sales model, allocate accordingly
  - Social: Brand awareness and community (allocate for reach goals)
  - Events: If part of strategy, allocate
  - Experimentation: 10-15% for testing new channels

*Step 4: ROI Modeling (4 hours)*
- For each major channel/campaign:
  - Estimate: Spend, impressions/reach, clicks, leads, SQL, customers
  - Model: Path to profitability and positive ROI timeline
  - Scenario: What if we increase/decrease budget by 25%?

*Step 5: Contingency Planning (2 hours)*
- What if budget is cut 20%? Which campaigns stay? Which get cut?
- What if we exceed goal by 20%? Where would we invest additional budget?
- Reallocation rules (how to shift budget during year if something underperforms)

**Budget Allocation Spreadsheet**:
- Tab 1: Annual budget by campaign
- Tab 2: Annual budget by channel
- Tab 3: Quarterly spend projections
- Tab 4: ROI modeling (spend → MQL → SQL → customer)
- Tab 5: Contingency scenarios

**Deliverables**:
- Budget Allocation Plan (2-3 pages): Summary of allocation, rationale, ROI expectations
- Detailed Budget Spreadsheet: All allocations, modeling, scenarios
- ROI Projection (1-2 pages): Expected performance, payoff timeline, break-even analysis
- Contingency Plan (1 page): Scenarios and response strategies

**Quality Criteria**:
- Budget allocated 100% (all monies accounted for)
- Each campaign has clear budget and ROI target
- Each channel has clear budget and performance expectations
- ROI modeling is realistic (based on historical performance or industry benchmarks)
- Contingency scenarios are defined

### 5. Audience Segmentation & Targeting (Audience Segmentation Specialist)

**Objective**: Define primary, secondary, and support audiences; understand their buying journey.

**Execution**:

**Primary Audience Definition** (8-10 hours):
Based on Phase 0 customer research and positioning, define your primary target audience in detail:

**Firmographic Data**:
- Company size (revenue, employees)
- Industries (vertical focus)
- Geography
- Tech stack
- Stage (startup, growth, established)

**Demographic Data**:
- Job titles (primary decision-maker)
- Experience level
- Functional area (marketing, sales, product, CS)
- Department

**Psychographic Data**:
- Goals and priorities
- Challenges and pain points
- Values and preferences
- Buy signals and indicators

**Behavioral Data**:
- How do they research solutions?
- Channels they frequent
- Content they consume
- Timing of buying cycle

**Buying Journey Mapping** (6-8 hours):
For primary audience, document complete buying journey:

**Awareness Stage**:
- How do they realize they have a need?
- Where do they research solutions?
- What content would help at this stage?
- Key metrics: Reach, impressions, engaged audience

**Consideration Stage**:
- What are they actively evaluating?
- What do they research? Who do they talk to?
- How does your solution fit?
- Key metrics: Demo requests, content engagement, sales conversations

**Decision Stage**:
- Who needs to approve? What's the process?
- What information do they need to buy?
- What could cause them to say no?
- Key metrics: Proposal sent, negotiation velocity, deal size

**Customer/Retention Stage**:
- How do they implement?
- What support do they need?
- How do we expand with them?
- Key metrics: Onboarding completion, time-to-value, expansion pipeline

**Secondary Audience Definition** (2-3 hours):
- Secondary audience: Different company size, industry, or use case
- How does buying journey differ?
- What messaging or content changes are needed?

**Audience Segmentation Model** (4-6 hours):
- Define 3-4 audience segments (by industry, company size, persona)
- For each segment: Firmographic, demographic, psychographic, behavioral data
- Buying journey variant: How does journey differ by segment?
- Messaging variant: What messaging resonates best?
- Channel strategy: Which channels reach this segment best?

**Deliverables**:
- Audience Definition Document (3-4 pages): Primary + secondary audiences, detailed characteristics
- Buying Journey Map (visual + 2-3 pages): Stage-by-stage view with content/timing needs
- Segment Profiles (2 pages per segment): For each key audience segment
- Audience Data Specification (1 page): How will we identify/target these audiences in CRM, ads platforms

**Quality Criteria**:
- Primary audience described in enough detail to target in ads platforms
- Buying journey mapped to marketing tactics (awareness → consideration → decision)
- Segment differences clear (not just list of attributes)
- Target audience size/TAM validated (realistic market opportunity)

### 6. Channel Strategy & Integration (Channel Strategy Planner)

**Objective**: Define how you will use each channel; ensure integration between channels.

**Execution**:

**Channel Assessment** (6-8 hours):
For each potential channel (Paid Search, LinkedIn, Facebook, Email, Blog, Content, Webinars, Outbound, Events, Partnerships):

- Reach: How many target audience members use this channel?
- Effectiveness: What engagement/conversion rates are typical?
- Cost: What's the typical cost per lead/customer for this channel?
- Maturity: How established is your presence? (Starting, growing, mature)
- Ownership: Who on the team runs this? (or do we need to hire/hire freelancer?)

**Channel Selection** (4 hours):
- Rank channels by: ROI potential, alignment with audience, resource availability, budget fit
- Select 4-5 primary channels for focus
- Identify 1-2 secondary channels to monitor
- Identify channels to pause (low ROI, misaligned)

**Channel Mix Definition** (4-6 hours):
For each primary channel, define:

**Paid Search** (if applicable):
- Keyword strategy: Which keywords? Branded/non-branded split?
- Budget: What's monthly spend?
- Performance target: Cost per lead? Conversion rate?
- Timeline: Seasonal variation?
- Creative: Ad copy themes?

**LinkedIn/Social Ads** (if applicable):
- Audience targeting: Who are we reaching?
- Creative strategy: What creative performs well?
- Cadence: How often are we posting/advertising?
- Budget and performance targets
- Integration: How does this support other channels?

**Email** (if applicable):
- List size and growth targets
- Cadence: How frequently are we emailing?
- Segmentation: Who gets what messages?
- Performance targets: Open rates, click rates, unsubscribe rates
- Integration with other channels (e.g., nurture website visitors)

**Content/Blog** (if applicable):
- Publishing frequency: Posts per month?
- Topics: What are we writing about?
- Format: Blog only? Plus gated content? Videos?
- SEO strategy: Keyword targeting?
- Distribution: How do we amplify content?

**Outbound** (if applicable):
- Target list: Who are we outreaching to?
- Approach: Cold email? LinkedIn? Phone?
- Messaging: What's the angle?
- Follow-up cadence: How many touches?
- Success metrics: Response rate? Meeting booking rate?

**Integrated Channel Plan** (2-3 hours):
- Map how channels work together
- Example: Paid Search drives website visitors → Email nurtures → Content educates → Outbound closes
- Identify hand-offs (how prospects move from one channel to next)
- Define metrics at each hand-off (conversion rates, timing)

**Deliverables**:
- Channel Strategy Document (4-5 pages): Assessment, selection, mix for primary channels
- Channel-Specific Strategies (1-2 pages per channel): Detailed approach for each primary channel
- Integrated Channel Flowchart (visual): How channels work together; customer journey
- Channel Performance Metrics (1-2 pages): KPIs and targets for each channel

**Quality Criteria**:
- 4-5 primary channels clearly defined with strategies
- Integration between channels documented (not siloes)
- Budget fits within overall allocation
- Performance metrics SMART and realistic
- Seasonal variation considered

## Phase 1 Quality Gate

**Approval Required From**: VP Marketing + Executive Sponsor (CMO)

**Review Criteria**:
- [ ] Positioning statement approved by exec team
- [ ] Positioning is differentiated (not generic; clear why we're different)
- [ ] Positioning validated with customer interviews (at least 3 customers give positive feedback)
- [ ] Message house developed with 3+ proof points
- [ ] Campaign architecture: 3-4 major campaigns defined with objectives, timelines, budgets
- [ ] Budget allocated across campaigns and channels (100% accounted for)
- [ ] Channel strategy includes 4-5 primary channels with specific tactics
- [ ] Audience segments defined with buying journey mapping
- [ ] Success metrics defined for campaigns and channels (SMART goals)
- [ ] Annual timeline/roadmap created with dependencies
- [ ] Sales alignment: Sales leadership has reviewed and agreed with positioning/messaging

**Remediation Process** (if gate not passed):
- Unclear positioning: Customer validation interviews to refine (3-5 days)
- Messaging not compelling: Iteration with team feedback (2-3 days)
- Budget not realistic: Remodeling with ROI assumptions or scope adjustment (2-3 days)
- Channel strategy gaps: Analysis of missing channels or over-allocation (2-3 days)

**Gate Sign-Off**:
Once approved, you have the strategy to move to Phase 2 (Foundation & Infrastructure).

## Common Phase 1 Pitfalls & Solutions

| Pitfall | Prevention | Solution |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Too many positioning ideas | Limit to 3-4 hypotheses | Use positioning workshop to narrow quickly |
| Messaging not validated with customers | Plan customer calls before finalizing | Get feedback from 3-5 customers before messaging lock |
| Campaign architecture too ambitious | Phase realistically (3-4 campaigns max) | Prioritize; move nice-to-haves to following year |
| Budget not defensible | Base on historical ROI + market opportunity | Use ROI model to show expected payoff |
| Channels not integrated | Review channel flows explicitly | Create visual showing how channels work together |
| Too many success metrics | Focus on 3-4 primary KPIs | Eliminate nice-to-haves; measure what matters |
