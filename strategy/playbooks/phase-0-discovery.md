# Phase 0: Discovery & Audit

**Duration**: 2-3 weeks for CATALYST-Full | 1-2 days for CATALYST-Sprint | Not typically used for CATALYST-Micro
**Agents Involved**: 8 discovery specialists
**Output**: Strategic foundation for all downstream marketing decisions

## Overview

Phase 0 establishes the factual foundation for your entire marketing strategy. Rather than assuming what your market, customers, and competitive landscape look like, you gather primary research and conduct systematic audits that surface opportunities and inform strategic positioning.

This phase answers critical questions:
- Who are we really trying to reach, and what do they care about?
- What do competitors do well, and where are the gaps?
- What's our actual market opportunity (TAM, SAM, SOM)?
- What technical SEO or marketing infrastructure issues exist?
- How do customers perceive us vs. competitors?

Phase 0 is not about perfect data—it's about directional truth. You're looking for patterns and insights that point toward positioning opportunities, not exhaustive analysis.

## Core Activities

### 1. Customer Insights Research (Customer Insights Researcher + Voice-of-Customer Specialist)

**Objective**: Understand who your target customers are, what they care about, and how they buy.

**Execution**:

**Primary Research** (10-12 interviews minimum)
- Conduct 30-45 minute interviews with 10-12 target customers
- Mix: 6-8 customers currently using your product, 2-3 prospects considering you, 2-3 customers of competitors
- Interview notes template captures: Company/role, pain points, buying process, solution approach, decision criteria
- Record interviews (with permission) or take detailed notes immediately after

**Research Topics**:
- Current situation: What brought them to evaluate solutions?
- Challenges and pain points: What's causing business impact?
- Solution approach: How are they currently solving this? What's working/not working?
- Evaluation criteria: How are they deciding between options?
- Internal process: Who influences/decides? What's the approval process?
- Timeline: What's the typical sales cycle?
- Success definition: How will they know if they made the right choice?

**Secondary Research** (10-15 hours)
- Review competitor reviews on G2, Capterra, Software Advice (what do customers praise/criticize?)
- Analyze LinkedIn insights for target audience (industries, company sizes, job titles)
- Read industry analyst reports if available (Gartner, Forrester, IDC)
- Monitor relevant subreddits, forums, Slack communities (what questions do customers ask?)
- Survey your sales team (what objections come up? What resonates?)

**Deliverables**:
- Customer research summary (2-3 pages): Key findings, patterns, themes
- Persona draft (one per major customer segment): Role, goals, challenges, buying process
- Quote library: 5-8 memorable customer quotes organized by theme

**Quality Criteria**:
- Minimum 10 customer interviews completed
- Personas include: Role, goals, challenges, buying process, success definition
- At least 2-3 quotes per persona directly from customer interviews
- Key themes identified across interviews (not one-off comments)

### 2. Competitive Intelligence Analysis (Competitive Intelligence Analyst)

**Objective**: Understand competitive landscape, positioning, and gaps.

**Execution**:

**Direct Competitor Analysis** (3-5 key competitors)
For each major competitor, document:

**Positioning & Messaging**:
- Website homepage messaging (value proposition, key claims)
- Product positioning (what problem do they solve?)
- Company narrative (where did they come from? Why do they exist?)
- Target customer messaging (who do they emphasize?)

**Go-to-Market Approach**:
- Paid advertising presence (search keywords they bid on, ad copy themes)
- Content strategy (blog topics, content types, publishing frequency)
- Social presence (channels used, engagement style, audience size)
- Customer testimonials and case studies
- Pricing and packaging approach
- Sales approach (sales team size indicators, self-serve vs. enterprise)

**Customer Perception**:
- G2 reviews: Rating, top praise, top criticism
- LinkedIn posts and employee activity
- Twitter/industry community sentiment
- Any public customer announcements

**Competitive Positioning Map**:
- Create 2-3 positioning matrices plotting competitors:
  - Example: Enterprise focus vs. Mid-market / Ease-of-use vs. Power
  - Look for white space (gaps in positioning)
  - Identify where you currently sit vs. where opportunity might be

**Deliverables**:
- Competitive summary (3-4 pages): 3-5 competitors analyzed with strengths, weaknesses, positioning
- Positioning map (visual): Shows where competitors sit; identifies gaps
- Messaging opportunity analysis (1 page): Where can you differentiate based on gaps?
- Customer perception summary: What do people say about competitive solutions?

**Quality Criteria**:
- 3-5 major competitors analyzed (not just direct but adjacent competitors too)
- Positioning map with clear axes and positioning clusters
- At least 2 distinct gaps or opportunities identified
- Customer feedback analysis from 20+ reviews minimum

### 3. SEO & Technical Audit (SEO Technical Auditor + Analytics Performance Analyst)

**Objective**: Identify current SEO health, technical opportunities, and competitive search landscape.

**Execution**:

**Website Technical Audit**:
- Crawl website using SEMrush or similar tool
- Check: Core Web Vitals (LCP, FID, CLS), mobile responsiveness, page load speed
- Review: Meta tags, heading structure, internal linking, robots.txt, sitemap.xml
- Identify: Broken links, duplicate content, non-indexed pages
- Check: Mobile rendering, structured data implementation, SSL certificate
- Document: Current domain authority, backlink profile, referring domains

**On-Page SEO Review**:
- Review top 20 pages for: Keyword optimization, headings, meta descriptions, internal links
- Check: Keyword density (naturally written, not stuffed), readability, formatting
- Identify: Missing/thin content opportunities, pages with high traffic but low engagement

**Competitive Search Landscape**:
- For 15-20 target keywords, analyze:
  - Search volume and keyword difficulty
  - Top 10 ranking websites (who dominates? how hard to compete?)
  - Featured snippets (what format wins)
  - Paid SERP ads (showing commercial intent)
  - Related searches (customer intent signals)

**Content Opportunity Mapping**:
- Identify keyword clusters (related terms that could be covered together)
- Map current content to keywords (what content addresses which keywords?)
- Identify gaps (keywords with search volume, no content ranking for them)

**Deliverables**:
- Technical audit report (3-4 pages): Issues, priority (critical/high/medium), recommendations
- Core Web Vitals assessment: Current scores, needed improvements, action plan
- Competitive SEO landscape (2 pages): Target keywords, competitive difficulty, opportunities
- Content opportunity matrix: 20-30 keyword opportunities with search volume, difficulty, recommended approach

**Quality Criteria**:
- Technical audit covers minimum 20-30 pages
- Keyword research includes 20+ target keywords with search volume data
- Competitive analysis includes top 10 ranking sites for each keyword
- Clear prioritization (quick wins vs. long-term investment)

### 4. Analytics & Performance Audit (Analytics Performance Analyst)

**Objective**: Understand current marketing performance and establish baseline metrics.

**Execution**:

**Website Performance Baseline**:
- Extract: Monthly traffic, traffic sources, top pages, bounce rate, average time on page
- Analyze: Device breakdown, geographic breakdown, user journey
- Review: Conversion paths (traffic source → pages visited → conversion)
- Compare: Month-over-month and year-over-year trends (if available)

**Campaign Performance History**:
- Pull: All paid campaigns (if any) with spend, clicks, conversions
- Analyze: Cost per click, conversion rate, cost per acquisition by channel
- Review: Email metrics (if sending) with open rates, click rates, unsubscribe rates
- Document: Organic search performance (if tracking)

**Lead Generation Audit** (if applicable):
- Total leads generated (monthly, by source)
- Lead quality assessment (conversion rate to opportunities)
- Sales cycle (time from lead to customer)
- Lead source attribution (which channels produce best customers?)

**Current Analytics Infrastructure**:
- Assessment: Is Google Analytics properly configured?
- Events: What conversion events are tracked?
- Conversions: How are leads/customers defined?
- Attribution: How is credit assigned to channels?
- Gaps: What should be tracked but isn't?

**Deliverables**:
- Marketing performance baseline (2-3 pages): Historical metrics, trends, current state
- Lead generation audit: Lead sources, quality, conversion rates
- Analytics infrastructure assessment: Current setup, gaps, recommendations
- Baseline metrics dashboard spec: KPIs to track going forward

**Quality Criteria**:
- Minimum 12 months of historical data (if available)
- Clear baseline metrics established for all channels
- Lead/customer definitions documented
- Infrastructure gaps identified with effort estimates for fixes

### 5. Market Sizing & Opportunity Assessment (Customer Insights Researcher + Industry Trend Analyst)

**Objective**: Understand total addressable market (TAM), serviceable market (SAM), and serviceable obtainable market (SOM).

**Execution**:

**TAM Calculation** (Multiple approaches for triangulation):

*Approach 1: Bottom-up*
- Define: Total number of target customer companies (by industry/geography/size)
- Estimate: % that have the problem you solve
- Estimate: Average annual spend on this category
- Result: TAM = (# target companies) × (% with need) × (average spend)

*Approach 2: Top-down*
- Find: Analyst reports on the category size (Gartner, Forrester, IDC if available)
- Adjust: For geographic scope, market segment
- Validate: Against bottom-up calculation

**SAM Calculation** (Serviceable Market)
- Define: Which customer segments can you realistically serve?
- Estimate: Total market within those segments
- Adjust: By geography, company size, vertical
- Result: SAM = TAM × (your segment focus)

**SOM Projection** (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
- Year 1: Realistic customer acquisition target (number of customers)
- Year 3: Growth projection (account for learning, scaling, market awareness)
- Year 5: Long-term vision

**Market Trends** (5-10 hours research):
- Identify: 3-5 major trends affecting the market
- Impact: How do trends help or hinder your positioning?
- Timeline: When will trends impact buying decisions?

**Deliverables**:
- Market sizing analysis (2-3 pages): TAM, SAM, SOM with methodology
- Market trend summary (1 page): Key trends, implications, timeline
- Opportunity assessment (1 page): Why now? Why you? What's the timing?

**Quality Criteria**:
- TAM calculated using at least 2 different methodologies
- Sources cited (analyst reports, government data, surveys, interviews)
- Clear distinction between TAM/SAM/SOM
- Reasonable growth projections for Year 1-3

### 6. Advertising & Sales Funnel Audit (Advertising Audit Analyst)

**Objective**: Understand current paid advertising performance and sales funnel health.

**Execution** (if running paid campaigns):

**Paid Advertising Audit** (if active):
- Google Ads: Account structure, keyword strategy, ad copy, landing pages, performance
- Social Ads (if active): Campaign setup, targeting, creative performance, landing pages
- Identify: Underperforming campaigns, underutilized audiences, underoptimized budget
- Review: Landing pages performance (bounce rate, conversion rate by source)

**Sales Funnel Analysis** (if have data):
- Define: Stages (awareness, consideration, decision, customer, advocate)
- Measure: Drop-off at each stage (% moving to next stage)
- Identify: Biggest bottleneck in your funnel
- Review: Time in each stage

**Messaging Resonance Audit**:
- Analyze: Current messaging (website, ads, emails)
- Assess: Does it resonate with customer insights from research?
- Identify: Gaps between what you say and what customers care about

**Deliverables**:
- Paid advertising audit (2-3 pages): Current performance, opportunities, recommendations
- Sales funnel health assessment: Stage conversion rates, bottleneck identification
- Messaging resonance analysis: Gaps between current messaging and customer insights

**Quality Criteria**:
- 6+ months of paid campaign data (if available)
- Clear ROI metrics established
- Funnel conversion rates documented
- 3-5 specific recommendations for improvement

## Phase 0 Quality Gate

**Approval Required From**: VP Marketing or CMO

**Review Criteria**:
- [ ] Market sizing complete and validated (TAM, SAM, SOM defined)
- [ ] Customer research includes interviews with 8-12 target buyers
- [ ] Competitive landscape mapped with 3-5 key competitors
- [ ] Personas draft-ready with role, goals, challenges defined
- [ ] Technical SEO audit completed with recommendations
- [ ] Historical marketing performance baseline established
- [ ] Market trends identified and implications noted
- [ ] Key assumptions documented for validation during optimization phase

**Remediation Process** (if gate not passed):
- Missing research: Prioritize urgent questions; skip nice-to-haves (3-5 days)
- Unclear findings: Re-interview or deep-dive on confusion (2-3 days)
- Insufficient analysis: Expand research in weak areas (3-5 days)

**Gate Sign-Off**:
Once approved, you have the foundation to move to Phase 1 (Strategy).

## Common Phase 0 Pitfalls & Solutions

| Pitfall | Prevention | Solution |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Analysis paralysis (too much research) | Set time boundaries (2-3 weeks max) | Prioritize directional truth over perfect data |
| Interviewing only happy customers | Plan interview sourcing upfront | Get contact list including lost deals, non-customers |
| Missing competitive nuance | Analyze 3-5 competitors minimum | Create positioning map to see competitive clusters |
| No baseline to measure against | Establish baseline Week 1 | Pull historical analytics/performance data early |
| Insights not actionable | Synthesize findings before gate | Make clear recommendations based on discoveries |
