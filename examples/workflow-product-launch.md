# Product Launch Workflow

## Overview

A comprehensive multi-agent workflow for launching a new B2B SaaS product feature or complete product. This workflow orchestrates 8+ specialized agents across positioning, messaging, content, design, paid media, and email over a 6-8 week period.

**Timeline**: 6-8 weeks from kickoff to launch
**Agents**: 8-10 specialized agents
**Parallel Tracks**: 3-4 concurrent work streams

---

## Phase 1: Discovery & Positioning (Weeks 1-2)

### Week 1: Research & Market Understanding

**Agents Involved**: Customer Insights Researcher, Competitive Intelligence, Product Positioning Strategist

#### Customer Insights Researcher
- **Input**: Product brief, target customer list
- **Activities**:
  - Conduct 8-12 customer interviews about the new feature/product
  - Analyze win/loss data related to this capability
  - Review G2, Capterra, and competitor reviews for related solutions
  - Document customer pain points, desired outcomes, and buying criteria
- **Deliverable**: Customer research synthesis document (15+ pages)
  - Verbatim customer quotes on why this capability matters
  - Job-to-be-done framework for target segments
  - Buying criteria ranked by importance
  - Decision-making process and stakeholder roles

#### Competitive Intelligence
- **Input**: Product brief, target market definition
- **Activities**:
  - Map competitive offerings for this capability
  - Document competitor positioning and messaging
  - Analyze competitor pricing and packaging
  - Identify market perception gaps and opportunities
  - Review third-party analyst reports and industry benchmarks
- **Deliverable**: Competitive positioning map
  - Competitive feature matrix
  - Competitor messaging analysis
  - White space opportunities identified
  - Market perception summary

#### Product Positioning Strategist
- **Input**: Customer research, competitive analysis, product brief
- **Activities**:
  - Run positioning workshop with product and marketing teams
  - Synthesize customer insights into market frame
  - Develop 2-3 positioning hypotheses to test
  - Create initial positioning narrative
  - Identify key proof points and evidence
- **Deliverable**: Positioning hypothesis document
  - Draft positioning statement
  - Market frame and category narrative
  - Initial messaging pillars (3-5 main themes)

---

### Week 2: Positioning Validation & Messaging Architecture

#### Product Positioning Strategist (continued)
- **Input**: Initial positioning hypothesis, sales team feedback
- **Activities**:
  - Test positioning with 5-8 customer advisory board members
  - Iterate based on feedback
  - Finalize positioning statement and market frame
  - Document category narrative in detail
- **Deliverable**: Final positioning framework document
  - Finalized one-sentence positioning statement
  - Category narrative (500 words)
  - Market frame visualization
  - Competitive positioning battle card

#### Messaging Architect
- **Input**: Final positioning framework
- **Activities**:
  - Develop comprehensive message architecture
  - Create 5-7 core message pillars with supporting submessages
  - Build proof point library with data, case studies, quotes
  - Document persona-specific messaging angles
  - Create messaging quick reference guide for all teams
- **Deliverable**: Complete messaging framework
  - Core message pillars with supporting messages
  - Proof point library (15-20 proof points)
  - Persona-specific messaging guide (CTO, CFO, VP Marketing, VP Sales)
  - Competitive objection handling messaging
  - 30-second elevator pitch variations

---

## Phase 2: Content & Asset Strategy (Weeks 2-4)

### Week 2-3: Content Planning (Parallel with Positioning)

#### Blog Strategist
- **Input**: Positioning framework, customer insights
- **Activities**:
  - Plan 4-6 blog posts around launch
  - Develop content calendar for launch week + 4 weeks post-launch
  - Define content themes and messaging integration
  - Plan how blog content connects to paid campaigns
- **Deliverable**: Blog content calendar and strategy
  - 4-6 blog post outlines with messaging integration
  - Publishing schedule
  - Content distribution plan
  - SEO strategy for launch keywords

#### Content Optimizer (SEO)
- **Input**: Product brief, target keywords, messaging
- **Activities**:
  - Research launch-related keywords and search intent
  - Optimize blog post outlines for SEO
  - Plan on-page SEO for product/feature landing page
  - Identify link-building opportunities
  - Plan technical SEO for new pages
- **Deliverable**: SEO optimization recommendations
  - Target keyword list with search volume and intent
  - On-page SEO checklist for landing page
  - Blog post SEO optimization brief
  - Internal linking strategy
  - Link acquisition strategy

#### Copywriter
- **Input**: Messaging framework, blog outlines, SEO requirements
- **Activities**:
  - Write 4-6 launch-related blog posts (1,500-2,500 words each)
  - Write hero landing page copy (headline, subheading, body)
  - Write email sequences (announcement, nurture, follow-up)
  - Ensure messaging consistency across all copy
- **Deliverable**: All copy deliverables
  - 4-6 blog articles (draft)
  - Landing page copy (all sections)
  - Email sequences (3-5 emails)
  - Supporting ad copy variations (10+ variations)

### Week 3-4: Design & Creative Assets

#### Landing Page Specialist
- **Input**: Landing page copy, messaging, positioning
- **Activities**:
  - Design landing page layout and structure
  - Plan conversion elements and CTAs
  - Design for mobile responsiveness
  - Plan A/B testing framework
- **Deliverable**: Landing page design and specification
  - Wireframes and high-fidelity designs
  - Conversion element recommendations
  - CTA copy variations
  - A/B testing plan (3-5 test hypotheses)

#### Content Visual Designer
- **Input**: Blog posts, positioning, brand guidelines
- **Activities**:
  - Create 8-12 graphics for blog posts
  - Design visual content for social media
  - Create infographics or data visualizations
  - Design supporting assets (one-pagers, checklists, templates)
- **Deliverable**: All visual assets
  - Blog graphics (2 per post)
  - Social media graphics (LinkedIn, Twitter, etc.)
  - One-pagers or worksheets
  - Visual proof points

#### Ad Creative Producer
- **Input**: Messaging, landing page, positioning
- **Activities**:
  - Design 6-8 ad creative variations
  - Design for multiple platforms (LinkedIn, Google, Facebook)
  - Plan creative testing framework
  - Design lead magnet or gated asset graphics
- **Deliverable**: Ad creative assets
  - LinkedIn ad creatives (image and carousel formats)
  - Google search and display ads
  - Facebook/Instagram ads
  - Lead magnet design
  - Ad copy variations

## Phase 3: Campaign Setup (Weeks 3-5)

### Week 3-4: Paid Media Strategy & Setup

#### PPC Strategist
- **Input**: Messaging, positioning, target audience, budget
- **Activities**:
  - Define campaign structure and naming convention
  - Develop keyword strategy (branded, competitive, intent-based)
  - Plan bid strategy and budget allocation
  - Design landing page strategy (which landing pages for which keywords)
  - Plan conversion tracking and attribution
- **Deliverable**: PPC campaign plan
  - Campaign structure document
  - Keyword research and grouping (300+ keywords)
  - Bid strategy recommendations
  - Landing page assignment matrix
  - Conversion tracking specifications

#### Budget Optimizer
- **Input**: Campaign goals, paid media plans, company budget constraints
- **Activities**:
  - Analyze historical campaign ROI by channel
  - Determine optimal budget allocation across channels
  - Set channel-specific targets (CAC, ROAS, pipeline value)
  - Plan budget pacing across launch phase
- **Deliverable**: Budget allocation plan
  - Total launch budget breakdown
  - Channel allocation (PPC: 35%, Social: 40%, Email: 5%, Design: 10%, etc.)
  - Weekly pacing plan
  - Target performance metrics by channel

#### Social Ads Specialist
- **Input**: Messaging, positioning, target audience, ad creatives
- **Activities**:
  - Define audience segments for targeting
  - Plan LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram ad strategy
  - Design account-based marketing ad strategy
  - Plan lookalike and retargeting audiences
  - Define conversion goals and tracking
- **Deliverable**: Social ads campaign plan
  - Campaign structure
  - Audience definitions and targeting criteria
  - Ad placement strategy
  - Lookalike and retargeting audience specifications
  - Conversion tracking setup

---

### Week 4-5: Email & Nurture Strategy

#### Email Lifecycle Architect
- **Input**: Messaging, positioning, target personas, product details
- **Activities**:
  - Design launch announcement email
  - Plan nurture sequence for launch period (3-week sequence)
  - Define trigger-based email flows
  - Plan how existing customers get notified
  - Define segmentation strategy for different audience types
- **Deliverable**: Email lifecycle map and sequences
  - Launch announcement email (copy and design)
  - 7-email nurture sequence (for leads acquired during launch)
  - Existing customer announcement email
  - Segmentation rules document
  - Automation workflow design

#### Email Copywriter
- **Input**: Messaging framework, email sequences from Email Lifecycle Architect
- **Activities**:
  - Write launch announcement subject lines and body copy (3 variations)
  - Write 7-email nurture sequence (including subject lines)
  - Write email supporting copy and CTAs
  - Ensure message consistency across sequences
- **Deliverable**: All email copy ready for platform
  - Launch announcement emails (3 variations)
  - 7-email nurture sequence (all copy)
  - Subject line variations (3 per email)
  - Pre-header text
  - CTA button text variations

---

## Phase 4: Orchestration & Launch Readiness (Weeks 5-6)

### Week 5: Launch Manager Coordination

#### Launch Manager
- **Input**: All strategy, creative, and campaign setup documents
- **Activities**:
  - Create comprehensive launch timeline
  - Identify all dependencies and critical path items
  - Schedule asset reviews and approval workflows
  - Coordinate cross-functional kickoff meeting
  - Set up launch war room and communication channels
  - Create launch readiness checklist
- **Deliverable**: Launch coordination documents
  - Detailed launch timeline (hour-by-hour for launch day)
  - Dependency map and critical path analysis
  - Stakeholder communication plan
  - Launch readiness checklist (100+ items)
  - Post-launch review schedule

#### Enablement Content Creator
- **Input**: Positioning, messaging, case studies, competitive objections
- **Activities**:
  - Create sales presentation deck (20-30 slides)
  - Develop competitive objection handling guide
  - Create customer-facing demo/walkthrough script
  - Prepare sales enablement training materials
  - Create sales collateral (one-pagers, battle cards, FAQs)
- **Deliverable**: Sales and customer enablement materials
  - Sales deck (30 slides) with speaker notes
  - Competitive objection guide
  - Product demo script
  - Sales training deck
  - Battle cards and objection responses
  - FAQ document

---

### Week 6: Final Review & Testing

#### All Teams
- **Activities**:
  - Review and approve all copy and creatives
  - Test all landing pages, forms, and email workflows
  - Conduct cross-functional launch readiness review
  - Load and QA all campaigns in platforms
  - Brief entire marketing and sales teams
  - Final messaging consistency audit

- **Deliverables**:
  - Sign-off documentation
  - QA test results
  - Team training completion logs
  - Final launch checklist with all items green

---

## Phase 5: Launch Execution (Week 7)

### Launch Day & Week 1

#### Launch Manager
- **Activities**:
  - Monitor campaign performance in real-time
  - Manage communications and escalations
  - Log all activities and incidents
  - Activate backup plans if needed
  - Coordinate across teams

#### PPC Strategist
- **Activities**:
  - Monitor bidding strategy and adjust as needed
  - Review quality score and landing page performance
  - Make real-time bid optimizations
  - Monitor conversion tracking accuracy

#### Social Ads Specialist
- **Activities**:
  - Monitor social campaign performance and CPM trends
  - Check audience fatigue and creative performance
  - Make real-time adjustments to targeting and budgets

#### Email Lifecycle Architect
- **Activities**:
  - Monitor email deliverability and engagement
  - Track automation workflow triggers
  - Monitor unsubscribe rates
  - Prepare follow-up sequences

#### Analytics/Pipeline Analyst
- **Input**: Live campaign data from all channels
- **Activities**:
  - Track real-time campaign performance against targets
  - Monitor lead generation and quality
  - Track early pipeline impact
  - Identify optimization opportunities
- **Deliverable**: Launch week performance report
  - Campaign performance by channel
  - Cost per lead by source
  - Lead quality assessment
  - Conversion rate analysis
  - Early ROI estimates

---

## Phase 6: Post-Launch Optimization (Weeks 8+)

#### All Agents
- **Activities**:
  - Review launch performance against goals
  - Implement optimizations based on learnings
  - Plan Phase 2 content and campaigns
  - Document lessons learned
  - Plan next product enhancements

## Success Metrics

### Campaign Performance
- **Lead Generation**: Target 200-500 MQLs during launch week
- **Cost Per Lead**: 15-20% below historical average
- **Lead Quality**: 30%+ SQLs (Sales Qualified Leads) within 2 weeks
- **Conversion Rate**: 3-5% on landing page (target visitor to lead)

### Content Performance
- **Blog Traffic**: 1,000+ visits to launch content within first week
- **Page Speed**: Sub-2 second load time on landing pages
- **Time on Page**: 2+ minutes average for blog content
- **Social Engagement**: 5%+ engagement rate on paid social

### Email Performance
- **Open Rate**: 30-35% on launch announcement
- **Click Through Rate**: 3-5% on CTAs
- **Delivery Rate**: 98%+ inbox delivery
- **Unsubscribe Rate**: <0.5%

### Sales Enablement
- **Sales Adoption**: 80%+ of sales team using positioning narrative in first 2 weeks
- **Deal Impact**: 20%+ faster deal progression for deals mentioning launch feature
- **Objection Handling**: Sales team confidence in responding to objections 4/5 or higher

### Business Impact
- **Pipeline Created**: $500K-$2M in pipeline influenced by launch (dependent on deal size)
- **Win Rate**: 2-3% improvement in win rate over 30 days post-launch
- **Customer Satisfaction**: NPS improvement for new feature users

## Critical Success Factors

1. **Start with Research** - Never begin positioning or messaging without customer research
2. **Cross-Functional Alignment** - Weekly sync meetings to coordinate across teams
3. **Clear Handoffs** - Each agent knows exactly what they're receiving and from whom
4. **Messaging Consistency** - Regular audits to ensure consistency across all channels
5. **Flexibility for Change** - Build 1-2 weeks of buffer for inevitable pivots
6. **Sales Involvement** - Get sales feedback early and often on messaging and assets
7. **Testing Mindset** - Plan A/B tests in paid media and email from the start
8. **Documentation** - Maintain detailed handoff documents at each stage

---

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Starting campaign setup before positioning is finalized
- Skipping customer research and building positioning on assumptions
- Creating assets before messaging framework is complete
- Running campaigns with inconsistent messaging across channels
- Underestimating timeline for design and creative reviews
- Not building buffer time for revisions and approvals
- Running campaigns without proper tracking and attribution
- Launching without sales team training and enablement
