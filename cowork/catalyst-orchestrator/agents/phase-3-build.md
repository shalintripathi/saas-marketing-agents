# Phase 3: Build & Content Production

**Duration**: 2-4 weeks for CATALYST-Full | 3-7 days for CATALYST-Sprint | Not typically used for CATALYST-Micro
**Agents Involved**: 9 content specialists (Blog Strategist, Blog Writer, Video Script Writer, Whitepaper Author, Case Study Producer, Email Content Specialist, Landing Page Specialist, Infographic Creator, Content Optimizer)
**Output**: Landing pages, blog content, ad creative, email sequences, videos, sales assets, case studies, whitepapers

## Overview

Phase 3 is where you create the assets that customers will see. You're producing:
1. **Demand generation assets**: Landing pages, forms, gated content
2. **Content marketing**: Blog posts, videos, whitepapers, case studies
3. **Paid advertising**: Ad copy, creative designs, landing pages
4. **Email marketing**: Nurture sequences, campaign emails, automation
5. **Sales enablement**: Sales decks, battlecards, case studies

Phase 3 is typically the heaviest resource phase. You're creating 50-100+ content pieces across multiple formats. The key is having clear templates, style guides, and processes to maintain quality and speed.

## Core Activities

### 1. Landing Page Strategy & Production (Landing Page Specialist)

**Objective**: Create high-converting landing pages for campaigns and lead generation.

**Execution**:

**Landing Page Audit** (if existing pages) (4-6 hours):
- Review current landing pages
- Analyze conversion rates (how many visitors convert to leads?)
- Identify underperforming pages (low conversion rate, low traffic)
- Identify best-performing pages (what works well?)

**Landing Page Planning** (8-10 hours):

Based on Phase 1 campaign architecture, plan landing pages:

**Campaign Landing Pages**:
- Campaign A Landing Page: Hero, value prop, form, CTA
- Campaign B Landing Page: Different messaging angle
- Campaign C Landing Page: Different audience variant

**Gated Content Landing Pages**:
- Case study landing page
- Whitepaper landing page
- ROI calculator landing page
- Demo/free trial landing page

**Webinar/Event Landing Pages**:
- Event registration page
- Thank you + replay page

**Total: 6-10 landing pages typically**

**Landing Page Design & Copy** (40-50 hours):

For each landing page:

**On-Page Elements**:
1. **Hero Section**
   - Headline (value prop in 5-8 words)
   - Subheading (supporting statement)
   - Hero image/video (eye-catching visual)
   - Primary CTA button

2. **Value Proposition**
   - What problem do you solve?
   - Why should they care?
   - Why you vs. alternatives?

3. **Proof/Credibility**
   - Customer testimonials (1-2 quotes)
   - Social proof (# customers, awards, media mentions)
   - Case study preview (results snapshot)
   - Security/compliance badges (if relevant)

4. **Features/Benefits**
   - 3-5 key features with benefit statements
   - Organized for scannability (bullets, icons)

5. **Call-to-Action/Form**
   - Form fields (minimize: Name, Email, Company)
   - Button text (action-oriented: "Get demo," "Download," etc.)
   - Post-submission: Thank you page, email confirmation

6. **Footer**
   - Links (privacy, terms, company info)
   - Trust signals (certifications, awards)
   - Copyright, contact info

**Copy Development**:
- Headline tested (proven to resonate with target audience)
- Copy benefits-focused (not feature-focused)
- Copy scannable (short paragraphs, bullets, subheadings)
- CTA clear and action-oriented
- Brand voice consistent (from Phase 2 brand guidelines)

**Design & Optimization**:
- Mobile responsive (works on phone, tablet, desktop)
- Fast loading (optimized images, clean code)
- Accessible (color contrast, alt text, keyboard navigation)
- Form optimization (minimizes fields, clear instructions)
- Visual hierarchy (most important info prominent)

**Landing Page QA Checklist**:
- [ ] All links functional (internal and external)
- [ ] Form submits successfully (test submission)
- [ ] Thank you page displays correctly
- [ ] Mobile rendering correct (tested on multiple devices)
- [ ] Images load quickly
- [ ] Spelling/grammar reviewed
- [ ] Brand guidelines followed
- [ ] CTA button is prominent and clear
- [ ] Form fields are labeled clearly
- [ ] Privacy/terms links present

**A/B Testing Setup**:
- Headline variants (Version A vs. Version B)
- CTA text variants
- Form field count variants (5 fields vs. 3 fields)

**Deliverables**:
- Landing Page Design Documents (1-2 pages per page): Layout, copy, design specs
- Landing Page HTML/Published Pages (live URLs)
- Landing Page QA Checklist (1 page): Pre-launch QA process
- A/B Testing Plan (1-2 pages): Variants to test, success metrics
- Analytics Integration (1 page): How to track conversions, setup details

**Quality Criteria**:
- Landing pages are mobile-responsive and fast-loading
- Copy benefits-focused and brand-consistent
- Forms are optimized (minimal fields, clear labels)
- CTAs are prominent and clear
- All pages are QA'd before launch

### 2. Blog Content Strategy & Production (Blog Strategist + Blog Writer)

**Objective**: Create SEO-optimized blog content to drive organic traffic and establish thought leadership.

**Execution**:

**Blog Content Planning** (6-8 hours):

From Phase 1 channel strategy and Phase 0 keyword research, plan blog topics:

**Topic Selection** (12-20 blog posts for CATALYST-Full):
- High-volume keywords (search volume 1,000+)
- Relevant to target audience (solves their problems)
- Owner opportunity (not dominated by major competitors)
- Aligned with marketing goals (drives awareness or consideration)

**Topics Organized by Funnel**:
- **Awareness**: "What is [topic]?", "How to [solve problem]", "[Problem] explained"
- **Consideration**: "How to choose [solution type]", "[Your solution] vs. [alternative]", "Best practices for [challenge]"
- **Decision**: "[Your product] pricing", "[Your product] reviews", "Why [your company]"

**Blog Calendar** (2 weeks for next month):
- 4 posts/week for CATALYST-Full (16 posts/month typically)
- 2 posts/week for CATALYST-Sprint
- Topics, publishing dates, responsible writers assigned

**Blog Post Production** (4-6 hours per post × 16-20 posts = 64-120 hours):

For each blog post:

**Research** (1 hour):
- Read 5-10 other articles on topic
- Gather data and statistics
- Compile sources for citations
- Develop unique angle or perspective

**Outline** (30 minutes):
- H1: Main heading (includes target keyword)
- H2s: 5-7 subheadings covering key points
- Bullet points under each section

**First Draft** (2-3 hours):
- Write 1,500-2,500 words typically
- Include target keyword naturally (3-5 times)
- Write benefits-focused, not product-focused
- Include 2-3 internal links to other content/products
- Include external citations (credibility)

**Editing & Optimization** (1.5 hours):
- Check for clarity and readability
- Scan for SEO optimization (keyword presence, headings, meta description)
- Check grammar and spelling
- Ensure brand voice consistent

**Meta Data**:
- Meta title (60 characters, includes keyword)
- Meta description (160 characters, compelling)
- Featured image (high-quality, relevant)
- Internal links (2-3 to related content)
- External links (2-3 to authoritative sources)

**Blog Post QA Checklist**:
- [ ] Keyword naturally integrated (3-5 times across 2,000 words)
- [ ] Headings use H2/H3 structure (no keyword stuffing)
- [ ] Meta title compelling and keyword-included
- [ ] Meta description is compelling call-to-action
- [ ] Featured image is high-quality and relevant
- [ ] Internal links (2-3) to related content
- [ ] External links (2-3) to authoritative sources
- [ ] Spelling/grammar reviewed
- [ ] Brand voice consistent
- [ ] Word count 1,500-2,500 words

**Blog Post Publishing**:
- Schedule via CMS with publication date
- Promote via email (send to email list)
- Promote via social media
- Monitor traffic and engagement

**Deliverables**:
- Blog Calendar (1-2 pages): Topics, dates, writers, status
- Blog Posts (16-20 published articles, 1,500-2,500 words each)
- Blog Post Template (1 page): Outline, format, QA checklist
- Blog Performance Tracking (1 page): Metrics to monitor (traffic, engagement, conversions)

**Quality Criteria**:
- Blog posts are 1,500+ words (substantial, SEO-friendly)
- Keyword research informs topic selection
- Content is benefits-focused and brand-consistent
- Posts are optimized for SEO (headings, internal links, meta data)
- Publishing schedule is consistent (regular cadence)

### 3. Email Content & Sequences (Email Content Specialist)

**Objective**: Create email nurture sequences and campaign emails that drive engagement and conversion.

**Execution**:

**Email Content Planning** (6-8 hours):

From Phase 1 customer journey mapping and Phase 2 automation setup, plan email content:

**Email Sequences**:

**Welcome Sequence** (3 emails over 1 week):
- Email 1: Welcome + set expectations
- Email 2: [3 days] Case study or social proof
- Email 3: [7 days] Special offer or next step

**Nurture Sequence - Awareness** (4 emails over 2 weeks):
- Email 1: Problem education (your challenge explained)
- Email 2: Solution overview (how your company solves it)
- Email 3: Case study (proof that it works)
- Email 4: CTA (next step: demo, trial, etc.)

**Nurture Sequence - Consideration** (6 emails over 4 weeks):
- Email 1: Feature education
- Email 2: Comparison (you vs. alternative)
- Email 3: ROI/pricing education
- Email 4: Customer testimonial
- Email 5: Objection handling
- Email 6: Strong CTA

**Campaign Emails** (per campaign):
- Campaign launch email (announce, exciting)
- Campaign follow-up (reminder, new angle)
- Campaign retargeting (for non-openers)

**Total emails**: 15-25 emails for CATALYST-Full

**Email Copy Development** (40-60 hours):

For each email:

**Subject Line** (critical; 30-60 characters):
- Action-oriented ("Discover how...", "See how...", "Learn...")
- Curiosity or benefit-driven
- Tested for highest open rate
- Examples: "3 ways to reduce churn 30%", "See why 500+ companies chose us"

**Email Body**:
- Greeting (personalized if possible)
- Hook (first 2-3 lines: why should they keep reading?)
- Body (benefit-focused, scannable, 150-300 words)
- CTA (clear, action-oriented)
- Signature (from name, company, optionally phone/LinkedIn)

**Email Design**:
- Responsive template (mobile-friendly)
- Brand colors and fonts (consistency)
- Single column for mobile optimization
- High contrast CTA button
- Clear unsubscribe link (CAN-SPAM requirement)

**Email Personalization**:
- Merge tags for name, company if available
- Dynamic content blocks for segment-specific messaging
- Timing optimization (send times vary by segment)

**Email QA Checklist**:
- [ ] Subject line is compelling and keyword/benefit included
- [ ] Email body is scannable (short paragraphs, line breaks)
- [ ] CTA is clear and action-oriented
- [ ] Email template is mobile-responsive
- [ ] Unsubscribe link present (CAN-SPAM compliance)
- [ ] Personalization merge tags correct
- [ ] Sender name clear
- [ ] Spelling/grammar reviewed
- [ ] Brand colors and fonts correct
- [ ] Images load correctly

**Email Automation Setup**:
- Configure triggers (what event sends this email?)
- Set timing (how many days after trigger?)
- Set segments (who receives this email?)
- Configure follow-ups (if no open, send reminder?)

**Deliverables**:
- Email Content Calendar (1-2 pages): Sequences, topics, status
- Email Templates (15-25 drafted emails with subject lines and body copy)
- Email Copy Guidelines (1 page): Best practices, brand voice, examples
- Email Performance Plan (1 page): Metrics to track, A/B testing plan

**Quality Criteria**:
- Subject lines are compelling and vary from email to email
- Email copy is scannable and benefits-focused
- Emails are mobile-responsive
- Unsubscribe/preference center link present
- Personalization implemented where possible
- Clear call-to-action in every email

### 4. Advertising Creative Production (Ad Creative Producer)

**Objective**: Create compelling ad creative for paid advertising campaigns.

**Execution**:

**Ad Creative Planning** (4-6 hours):

From Phase 1 channel strategy and Phase 3 landing pages, plan ad creative:

**Ad Formats Needed**:
- **Search ads** (Google Ads): Headline + description text
- **Social ads** (LinkedIn, Facebook): Image + headline + body + CTA
- **Display ads** (retargeting): Banner ads (various sizes)

**Creative Variations**:
- 3-5 variants per campaign (for A/B testing)
- Different value propositions tested
- Different visuals tested (if image-based)
- Different CTAs tested

**Total ads**: 20-40 ad variants for CATALYST-Full

**Search Ad Creation** (2 hours per ad × 8-10 ads):

Google Ads format (headlines + descriptions):

**Ad Structure**:
- **Headline 1** (30 characters): Keyword or value prop
- **Headline 2** (30 characters): Supporting claim or benefit
- **Headline 3** (30 characters): CTA or urgency
- **Description Line 1** (90 characters): Benefit or feature
- **Description Line 2** (90 characters): Social proof or CTA

**Best Practices**:
- Include target keyword in headline (improves Quality Score)
- Benefit-focused copy (not feature-focused)
- Clear CTA (Download, Sign Up, Get Demo, Learn More)
- Address pain points (what problem do we solve?)
- Include numbers/proof when possible (30% faster, 5-star rating)

**Example**:
- Headline 1: "Reduce Customer Churn"
- Headline 2: "AI-Powered Retention"
- Headline 3: "See Your Demo"
- Description 1: "Predict churn 90 days early. Take action before you lose them."
- Description 2: "Join 500+ companies saving millions with smarter retention strategies."

**Social Ad Creation** (3-4 hours per ad × 12-20 ads):

LinkedIn/Facebook format (image + text):

**Image Requirements**:
- High quality (no blurry or low-res images)
- On-brand (use brand colors, fonts from Phase 2)
- Relevant to message (if advertising retention, show happy customers)
- Simple composition (not too busy)
- Text overlay optional (not more than 20% of image)

**Image Sourcing**:
- Stock photos (Unsplash, Pexels for free; iStock, Shutterstock for premium)
- Original photography (if you have)
- Graphic design (Canva templates if budget limited)
- Video thumbnail (if using video ads)

**Ad Copy for Social**:
- Headline (5-10 words, benefit or curiosity)
- Body copy (50-100 words, conversational tone)
- CTA (Learn More, Sign Up, Get Demo, etc.)
- URL (linked to landing page)

**Example LinkedIn Ad**:
- Image: Happy customer using software
- Headline: "Reduce Churn by 30%"
- Body: "Stop losing customers. Our AI predicts churn 90 days early so you can take action. See how 500+ companies save millions."
- CTA: "Watch Demo"
- URL: Landing page

**Display/Retargeting Ads** (2-3 hours per ad × 4-6 ads):

Small banners for retargeting (various sizes: 300x250, 728x90, 160x600):

**Design Approach**:
- Reminder of value prop
- Usually includes company logo
- CTA button
- Simple, clean design (not cluttered)

**Ad Performance Tracking Setup**:
- UTM parameters on each ad (track which ad drove traffic)
- Conversion tracking (track which ads lead to conversions)
- A/B testing framework (which variants perform best?)

**Deliverables**:
- Ad Creative Plan (2 pages): Formats, messages, variants planned
- Search Ads (8-10 ads drafted in Google Ads format)
- Social Ads (12-20 ads with images and copy)
- Display Ads (4-6 banner designs)
- Ad Performance Tracking Plan (1 page): UTM strategy, A/B test plan, metrics

**Quality Criteria**:
- Ads are on-brand (colors, fonts, voice consistent)
- Copy is benefit-focused and compelling
- Headlines are 5-10 words (scannable)
- Multiple variants prepared for A/B testing
- Images are high-quality and relevant
- CTAs are clear and action-oriented
- UTM parameters consistent and documented

### 5. Sales Enablement Assets (Sales Asset Creator)

**Objective**: Create assets that help sales team close deals.

**Execution**:

**Sales Enablement Planning** (4-6 hours):

From Phase 1 messaging and Phase 3 content, create sales assets:

**Asset Types**:
- **One-pagers**: Quick overview of product/solution
- **Competitive battlecards**: How to position against competitors
- **Case studies**: Customer success stories with results
- **ROI calculator**: Help prospects understand financial impact
- **Sales deck**: Comprehensive product overview
- **FAQ/Objection handling**: Common questions and answers
- **Proposal templates**: Customizable proposals
- **Feature comparison**: Your product vs. alternatives

**Sales Asset Creation** (20-30 hours):

**One-Pager Template**:
- Company logo, value prop
- 3-5 key benefits (bullets)
- Customer testimonial or stat
- How it works (visual diagram)
- CTA and contact info

**Case Study Template**:
- Customer name, industry, size
- Challenge they faced (pain point)
- Solution you provided
- Results achieved (quantified: revenue, time, efficiency gains)
- Quote from customer
- Contact/reference information

**Competitive Battlecard Template**:
- Competitor name and positioning
- How they position themselves
- Your differentiation (why you're better)
- Talking points (quotes to use in conversations)
- Questions to ask prospects about the competitor

**Sales Deck Template**:
- Title slide
- Problem statement (customer's challenge)
- Why it matters (business impact)
- Your solution overview
- How you're different (competitive positioning)
- Key features + benefits
- Customer success stories (case studies)
- Pricing/investment (if sharing)
- Next steps/CTA
- Contact information

**ROI Calculator**:
- Inputs: Company size, current situation (churn, revenue, etc.)
- Calculation: Annual savings or revenue impact
- Output: Customized ROI for their situation

**Implementation**:
- Create in Google Docs, Canva, or PowerPoint
- Make shareable (share link, not just attachment)
- Make customizable (sales team fills in customer name, details)
- Make up-to-date (update quarterly)

**Deliverables**:
- One-Pagers (3-5 covering different aspects/use cases)
- Case Studies (3-5 documented customer successes)
- Competitive Battlecards (for 3-5 major competitors)
- Sales Deck Template (customizable, 15-20 slides)
- ROI Calculator (Google Sheet or similar)
- Sales Enablement Pack (1-2 pages): What assets exist, how to use them

**Quality Criteria**:
- All assets are on-brand and professional-looking
- Copy is benefit-focused and customer-centric
- Assets are up-to-date and accurate
- Customizable for sales team use
- Case studies have specific, quantified results

### 6. Video Content & Scripts (Video Script Writer)

**Objective**: Create video content for awareness, education, and conversion.

**Execution**:

**Video Content Planning** (4-6 hours):

Types of videos needed:

**Educational Videos** (3-5 minutes):
- Problem education (explaining your customer's challenge)
- Solution overview (how your product works)
- Feature explanation (specific features explained)
- Best practices (how to achieve results)

**Customer Testimonials** (1-2 minutes):
- How customer found you
- Problems you solved
- Results achieved
- Recommendation

**Case Study Videos** (2-3 minutes):
- Company overview
- Their challenge
- Your solution
- Results and impact

**Webinar/Training** (20-45 minutes):
- Educational content on a topic
- Includes slides, speaker, interaction
- Recorded and repurposed into smaller clips

**Demo/Product Walk-Through** (3-5 minutes):
- How your product works
- Key features overview
- How to get started

**Total videos**: 5-10 for CATALYST-Full (mix of lengths)

**Video Script Development** (3-5 hours per script × 7-10 scripts):

For each video:

**Script Structure**:
- **Hook** (0-5 seconds): Grab attention, state value
- **Problem** (5-15 seconds): Describe the challenge/opportunity
- **Solution** (10-20 seconds): Explain your solution
- **Proof** (5-10 seconds): Share evidence (stats, testimonial, result)
- **Call-to-action** (5 seconds): Next step

**Script Format**:
- 80-100 words per minute (roughly; depends on pace)
- Conversational tone (not robotic)
- Short sentences (easy to speak)
- Include visual directions (what's on screen when?)

**Example Script** (2-minute video):

```
[0-5 sec] HOOK
Narrator: "Losing customers costs you millions every year. But what if you could predict and prevent churn?"

[5-15 sec] PROBLEM
Narrator: "Most companies react to churn too late. By the time you know someone's leaving, they're already gone. That costs time, money, and growth."

[15-35 sec] SOLUTION
Narrator: "Our AI predicts churn 90 days in advance. So you can reach out before they leave. Offer discounts, new features, or personal attention—whatever works for your situation."

[35-45 sec] PROOF
Narrator: "Companies using our platform reduce churn by 30% on average. That's millions in recovered revenue."
[Show case study on screen]

[45-50 sec] CTA
Narrator: "Ready to save customers you'd otherwise lose? Book a demo today."
[Show demo link on screen]
```

**Video Production**:
- Screen recording software (Loom, Camtasia)
- Speaking talent (founder, spokesperson, or professional voice actor)
- Editing software (Adobe Premiere, iMovie, or use editor)
- Captions (required for accessibility and engagement)
- Thumbnail design (if posting to YouTube)

**Deliverables**:
- Video Script Documents (1-2 pages per video)
- Video Production Plan (1-2 pages): Which videos to produce, timeline, resources needed
- Produced Videos (5-10 videos, various lengths)
- Video Performance Plan (1 page): Where videos will be posted, how to measure success

**Quality Criteria**:
- Scripts are conversational and benefit-focused
- Videos have clear hook and CTA
- Captions are accurate and synced
- Video quality is professional
- Thumbnails are attention-grabbing (if YouTube)

## Common Phase 3 Pitfalls & Solutions

| Pitfall | Prevention | Solution |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Taking too long on perfection | Set word counts and time limits per piece | Focus on "good enough to ship"; optimize after launch |
| Not enough content variety | Plan different formats (blog, video, email, ads) | Prioritize highest-ROI formats; lower-priority formats can wait |
| Copy not aligned with messaging | Use Phase 1 messaging documents as reference | Daily copywriting huddle to align across team |
| Landing page conversion rates too low | Test headlines and CTAs before launch | A/B test top 3 landing pages during Phase 4 |
| Email sequences not activated | Set up sequences in email platform while writing | Parallel track: writing and configuration |
| Sales assets incomplete | Get sales team input on what they need | Gather feedback early; iterate quickly |
