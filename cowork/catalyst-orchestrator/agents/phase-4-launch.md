# Phase 4: Launch & Activation

**Duration**: 1 week for CATALYST-Full | 2-3 days for CATALYST-Sprint | Not typically used for CATALYST-Micro
**Agents Involved**: 7 activation specialists (PPC Strategist, LinkedIn Ads Specialist, Facebook/Instagram Ads Specialist, Launch Manager, Outbound Specialist, Email Specialist, Social Lead)
**Output**: Live campaigns across all channels, verified tracking, initial performance baseline

## Overview

Phase 4 is where all the planning and production comes to life. You're activating campaigns simultaneously across multiple channels, monitoring initial performance, and establishing baseline metrics.

Phase 4 is high-paced and high-stakes. You're:
1. **Launching paid campaigns**: Setting up Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads, and other paid channels
2. **Deploying email**: Sending welcome sequences and campaign emails
3. **Activating outbound**: Starting cold email and LinkedIn outreach
4. **Promoting content**: Sharing blog content across social and email
5. **Running social campaigns**: Posting content and running social ads
6. **Monitoring performance**: Tracking data, confirming tracking accuracy, adjusting as needed

The key to Phase 4 success is coordination. You want multiple channels going live within 48 hours, so you understand the blended performance. This prevents scenarios where "email drove all the leads" when actually three channels contributed.

## Core Activities

### 1. Paid Search Activation (PPC Strategist)

**Objective**: Launch Google Ads campaigns to drive traffic and leads.

**Execution**:

**Google Ads Setup** (4-6 hours):

**Account Structure**:
- Campaign per major initiative (Campaign A, B, C from Phase 1)
- Ad group per audience segment or message variant
- Ad set per landing page or offer

**Example Structure**:
- Campaign: "Q1 Launch - Awareness"
  - Ad Group: "Segment A - Value Prop 1"
  - Ad Group: "Segment A - Value Prop 2"
  - Ad Group: "Segment B - Value Prop 1"

**Keyword Strategy**:
- **Branded keywords** (your company name, variations)
  - Volume: 20-50/month typically
  - Intent: Very high (people looking for you)
  - Bid: Higher (prevent competitors from outbidding)

- **Category keywords** (generic terms in your space)
  - Volume: 50-200+/month typically
  - Intent: Medium-high (people looking for solutions)
  - Bid: Medium

- **Problem keywords** (keywords indicating need)
  - Volume: Varies (often high volume)
  - Intent: Medium (people have the problem)
  - Bid: Medium-low

**Keywords to Target** (30-50 keywords typically):
- Branded: 5-10 keywords (your company and variations)
- Category: 10-20 keywords (product category, solutions)
- Problem: 10-20 keywords (customer pain points)

**Bidding Strategy**:
- Start with manual CPC (you control bid amount)
- Bid higher for branded keywords ($3-5 per click)
- Bid medium for category keywords ($1-3 per click)
- Bid lower for problem keywords ($0.50-2 per click)
- Use bid adjustments (increase bids during business hours, decrease nights/weekends)

**Campaign Setup** (6-8 hours):

For each campaign:

**Campaign Settings**:
- Budget: Daily budget (total spend / 30 days) = daily budget
- Bidding strategy: Conversion-based (if possible) or manual CPC
- Ad rotation: Optimize for conversions (show best-performing ads more)
- Ad schedule: When to show ads (business hours if B2B)
- Geographic targeting: Where should ads show? (all regions, or targeted?)
- Audience targeting: Remarketing audiences (retarget site visitors)

**Ad Group Setup**:
- Keywords: 8-12 keywords per ad group (tightly themed)
- Ads: 2-3 ad variants per ad group
- Landing page: One landing page per ad group

**Landing Page Alignment**:
- Ad promises [X benefit]
- Landing page delivers on [X benefit]
- No disconnect (ad says "30% faster," landing page should emphasize speed)

**Conversion Tracking** (2-3 hours):

Set up tracking to measure results:

**Conversions to Track**:
- Form submission (lead generation)
- PDF download (engagement)
- Video view (engagement)
- Thank you page visit (form completion)
- Revenue (if tracking customers)

**Tracking Implementation**:
- Install conversion pixel (Google Ads provides code)
- Install via Google Tag Manager (recommended)
- Test conversion tracking (submit form, verify conversion fires)

**Launch Checklist**:
- [ ] Keywords researched and selected (30-50 keywords)
- [ ] Ad copy written and approved (2-3 variants per ad group)
- [ ] Landing pages live and conversion tracking verified
- [ ] Campaigns structured logically
- [ ] Daily budget set appropriately
- [ ] Bid amounts entered
- [ ] Ad rotation optimized for conversions
- [ ] Conversion tracking set up and tested
- [ ] Campaign approved by team lead
- [ ] Campaign set to "Enabled" (ready to run)

**Launch**:
- Set campaign to live
- Monitor for first 4 hours (check if clicks/impressions flowing)
- Check ad approval status (Google reviews ads, usually approves within few hours)

**Initial Performance Monitoring** (ongoing daily):
- Impressions (are we showing up in search results?)
- Clicks (are people clicking our ads?)
- Click-through rate (what % of impressions become clicks?)
- Conversion rate (what % of clicks convert to leads?)
- Cost per conversion (how much does each lead cost?)

**Deliverables**:
- Google Ads Campaign Setup Document (3-4 pages): Campaign structure, keywords, bids
- Ad Copy Document (1-2 pages): All ads written out
- Launch Checklist (1 page): Pre-launch verification
- Performance Monitoring Plan (1 page): Daily metrics to track, targets

**Quality Criteria**:
- Keywords are relevant to landing pages and value propositions
- Ad copy is compelling and matches landing pages
- Conversion tracking is set up and tested
- Campaign is properly structured and budgeted
- Initial impressions/clicks flowing within first hours

### 2. LinkedIn Ads & Social Ads Activation (LinkedIn Ads Specialist + Facebook/Instagram Ads Specialist)

**Objective**: Launch LinkedIn and social media advertising campaigns.

**Execution**:

**LinkedIn Ads Setup** (4-6 hours):

**Campaign Objective** (what do you want to happen?):
- Website visits (drive traffic to landing pages)
- Lead generation (collect leads via LinkedIn form)
- Engagement (get comments, shares on LinkedIn)
- Video views (promote video content)

**Audience Targeting**:
- Job titles (VP Marketing, CMO, Head of Sales, etc.)
- Industries (software, professional services, etc.)
- Company sizes (100-500 employees, 500-1000, etc.)
- Company names (target decision-makers at specific companies)
- Behaviors (job changers, recent promotions, etc.)

**Audience Size**:
- Recommended: 50K-500K audience size (not too broad, not too narrow)
- Test with 1-2 audience segments first

**Campaign Creative**:
- Single image ads (most common): Image + headline + body + CTA
- Carousel ads (multiple images): Show different value props
- Video ads: Auto-play video with sound off
- Lead gen ads: Click ad → LinkedIn form auto-populates

**Budget Allocation**:
- Daily budget: $20-50/day typical for testing
- Bid strategy: Automatic bids initially (let LinkedIn optimize)
- Campaign duration: 2-4 weeks minimum to gather data

**Campaign Launch**:
1. Create audience segment
2. Upload creative (image or video)
3. Write ad copy (headline, body, CTA)
4. Set daily budget
5. Set targeting parameters
6. Review and launch

**Facebook/Instagram Ads Setup** (4-6 hours):

**Campaign Objectives**:
- Traffic (drive website visits)
- Conversions (drive form submissions or signups)
- Lead generation (Facebook lead form)
- App installs (if mobile app)
- Video views

**Audience Targeting**:
- Interests (marketing tools, business software, etc.)
- Behaviors (people interested in business productivity)
- Custom audiences (if you have customer email list)
- Lookalike audiences (people similar to your customers)
- Saved audiences (create and save for re-use)

**Creative Development**:
- Single image ads: Image + headline + body + CTA
- Carousel ads: Multiple images in sequence
- Video ads: Auto-play video on news feed
- Collection ads: Product catalog showcase

**Budget & Bidding**:
- Daily budget: $20-50/day typical for testing
- Bid strategy: Lowest cost (Meta optimizes for conversions)
- Audience network: Include Messenger, Audience Network (broader reach)

**Tracking & Pixel**:
- Install Facebook Pixel on website
- Set up conversion tracking (form submission, page visit, etc.)
- Create custom conversions for events (like "demo requested")

**Social Ads Best Practices**:
- Mobile-first design (most viewing on mobile)
- Video-based creative (higher engagement than images)
- Short video (3-15 seconds, auto-play with sound off)
- Single primary CTA (one action per ad)
- A/B test creative variants
- Target narrow audiences (specific enough to be relevant)

**Deliverables**:
- LinkedIn Campaign Setup (2-3 pages): Audiences, creative, messaging
- Facebook/Instagram Campaign Setup (2-3 pages): Audiences, creative, messaging
- Ad Creative Assets (images, videos, copy)
- Launch Checklist (1 page): Pre-launch verification for each platform
- Performance Monitoring Plan (1 page): Daily metrics to track

**Quality Criteria**:
- Audiences are well-defined and properly sized
- Ad creative is mobile-optimized
- Copy is benefit-focused and action-oriented
- Conversion tracking set up and verified
- Multiple ad variants created for A/B testing

### 3. Email Launch & Deployment (Email Specialist)

**Objective**: Deploy email sequences and campaign emails.

**Execution**:

**Email Sequence Activation** (2-4 hours):

**Pre-Launch Checklist**:
- [ ] All emails written and approved
- [ ] Email templates styled and tested
- [ ] Merge tags tested (name personalization works)
- [ ] Unsubscribe link present on all emails
- [ ] Sender name and reply-to email set correctly
- [ ] Spam testing completed (email not flagged as spam)
- [ ] All links functional
- [ ] Images load correctly
- [ ] Mobile rendering correct
- [ ] CTA button prominent and functional

**Welcome Sequence Activation**:
- Activation trigger: New contact created or marked as lead
- Email 1: Send immediately (within 1 hour)
- Email 2: Send 3 days after Email 1
- Email 3: Send 7 days after Email 1
- Monitor: Open rates, click rates, unsubscribe rates

**Nurture Sequence Activation**:
- Activation trigger: Contact enters segment or downloads gated content
- Email cadence: 1 email every 3-5 days
- Duration: 4-12 weeks depending on sequence
- Monitor: Engagement by segment, conversion rates by sequence

**Campaign Email Sends**:
- Schedule: Send at optimal time (typically 10am or 2pm on weekdays)
- Segment: Send only to relevant audience segments
- Frequency cap: Not more than 1 email per day to same person
- Pre-send test: Send test to team members, verify rendering

**Launch Steps**:
1. Set sequence trigger and timing
2. Send test email to team members
3. Verify personalization works correctly
4. Verify conversion tracking fires correctly
5. Set sequence to active
6. Monitor first sends for issues

**Performance Monitoring**:
- Open rate (% of recipients who open)
- Click rate (% of recipients who click)
- Unsubscribe rate (% of recipients who unsubscribe)
- Conversion rate (% who convert after email)
- Bounce rate (% invalid email addresses)

**Troubleshooting**:
- Low open rate: Improve subject lines
- Low click rate: Improve CTA prominence and copy
- High unsubscribe rate: Reduce frequency or relevance of emails
- High bounce rate: Clean email list, remove invalid addresses

**Deliverables**:
- Email Launch Plan (2 pages): Sequences, triggers, timing
- Pre-Launch Checklist (1 page): All verification items
- Email Performance Tracking (1 page): Metrics to monitor

**Quality Criteria**:
- All emails tested and rendering correctly
- Unsubscribe link present on all emails
- Personalization merge tags working
- Conversion tracking verified
- Sequences activated and sending

### 4. Outbound Activation (Outbound Specialist)

**Objective**: Launch cold email and LinkedIn outreach sequences.

**Execution** (if applicable to your business model):

**Outbound List Building** (6-8 hours):

**List Sourcing**:
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator (if B2B, targeting specific companies)
- Apollo.io or Hunter.io (email finder tools)
- Clearbit or similar (company data enrichment)
- Manual research (for high-priority accounts)

**List Criteria**:
- Target title: VP Marketing, CMO, VP Sales, etc. (decision-makers)
- Company size: Align with your target market
- Industry: If you have vertical focus
- Geography: Where you want to focus
- List size: 100-500 prospects to start (test before scaling)

**List Verification**:
- Email validation: Run through email verification tool (reduce bounces)
- Duplicate removal: Remove duplicate contacts
- Opt-out checks: Don't email people who've opted out

**Outbound Sequence Setup** (4-6 hours):

**Email Sequence** (cold email approach):
- Email 1 (Day 0): Introduction, problem/solution angle, soft CTA
- Email 2 (Day 3): Different angle or proof point, CTA for meeting
- Email 3 (Day 7): Scarcity/urgency ("This offer closes X date")
- Email 4 (Day 10): Final attempt, low-pressure CTA
- If no response, move to LinkedIn

**LinkedIn Outreach**:
- Message 1 (Day 5): Connection request + short personalized note
- Message 2 (Day 12, if connected): Conversation starter + value prop
- Message 3 (Day 15): Specific ask (demo, call, resource)

**Sequence Timing**:
- Spread over 2-3 weeks
- Multiple touchpoints increase response rate
- Mix email and LinkedIn for better reach

**Personalization**:
- Merge tags for name: "Hi [first_name]"
- Merge tags for company: "I noticed [company_name]..."
- Personalized angles: Reference recent funding, job change, news

**Copy Guidelines**:
- Subject lines: Short, benefit-focused, curiosity-driven
- Email body: 50-100 words (short and scannable)
- Casual tone (not formal/salesy)
- Clear CTA (demo, 15-min call, resource)

**Example Sequence**:

*Email 1*:
- Subject: "Quick question about [company_name]'s churn strategy"
- Body: "Hi [first_name], I was researching [company_name] and saw you're the VP of Marketing. We just helped [similar company] reduce churn by 30%. Curious if that's something you're working on? [Demo link]"

*Email 2*:
- Subject: "RE: Quick question about [company_name]'s churn strategy"
- Body: "Following up on my last note. Most of our customers see ROI in 30 days. Would a quick 15-min call make sense? [Calendar link]"

**Outbound Tool Setup** (2-3 hours):
- Email sequencing tool: Outreach, Salesloft, or Lemlist
- Configure: Template, timing, merge tags
- Test: Send test sequence to yourself, verify personalization
- Launch: Set to active

**Launch**:
1. Upload list of prospects
2. Configure merge tags
3. Set start date and timing
4. Send test sequence
5. Launch to full list

**Performance Tracking**:
- Open rate (what % open the email?)
- Reply rate (what % respond?)
- Meeting booked rate (what % book a meeting?)
- Response time (how long to get replies?)

**Deliverables**:
- Outbound Strategy Document (2-3 pages): List, sequencing, approach
- Email Sequences (2-3 pages): All email copy outlined
- LinkedIn Sequence Plan (1 page): Connection approach, messaging
- Launch Checklist (1 page): Pre-launch verification

**Quality Criteria**:
- List is clean and properly segmented
- Email sequences are personalized
- Copy is casual and benefit-focused
- Tool is configured and tested
- Tracking set up to measure results

### 5. Content Promotion & Social (Social Lead)

**Objective**: Promote content across social media and owned channels.

**Execution**:

**Content Distribution Strategy** (2-3 hours):

**Distribution Channels**:
- Email (newsletter to existing list)
- LinkedIn (company page + organic posts)
- Twitter/X (if audience present)
- Facebook/Instagram (if audience present)
- Paid social (amplify top content)
- Industry communities (relevant forums, Slack groups, Reddit)

**Publishing Schedule**:
- Blog posts: Publish to blog, then promote day of publication
- Email: Send newsletter 1-2x per week with new content links
- LinkedIn: Post 3-5x per week (mix own content + industry content)
- Twitter: Post 1-2x per day (more frequent for real-time engagement)
- Paid boost: Spend $200-500/month boosting top-performing content

**Content Promotion Template**:

*Blog Post Launch*:
- Day 0: Email newsletter ("Check out our latest article on X")
- Day 0: LinkedIn post (article summary + link)
- Day 1: Twitter thread (key points from article)
- Day 2: LinkedIn followup (different angle on same content)
- Day 3-5: Boost with paid social (if content resonates)

**Social Content Calendar** (1-2 pages):
- Dates and topics for next 4 weeks
- Content format (blog, video, infographic, etc.)
- Distribution channels
- Owner/responsible person

**Organic Social Best Practices**:
- LinkedIn: Share insights, ask questions, engage with others
- Twitter: Industry news, quick tips, engage in conversations
- Facebook: Stories and community engagement (if B2C focus)
- Cadence: Post 3-5x per week minimum for visibility

**Paid Social Promotion** (2-3 hours):
- Create ads promoting best-performing content
- Target: Similar audience to paid ads but cheaper
- Budget: $200-500/month for content promotion
- Measure: Traffic, engagement, conversions from promoted content

**Community Engagement** (ongoing):
- Monitor mentions of your brand on Twitter, LinkedIn, Reddit
- Respond to comments and questions
- Engage in industry discussions
- Build relationships and visibility

**Deliverables**:
- Social Media Strategy (2 pages): Channels, posting frequency, content types
- Content Promotion Calendar (2-3 pages): What's being promoted, when, where
- Paid Social Plan (1 page): Budget, content types, target metrics

**Quality Criteria**:
- Content promoted consistently across channels
- Cadence is sustainable (team can maintain)
- Posts are engaging and drive interaction
- Paid budget allocated to highest-value content
- Community engagement is responsive

### 6. Campaign Launch Coordination (Launch Manager)

**Objective**: Coordinate all simultaneous activations and monitor initial performance.

**Execution**:

**Pre-Launch Coordination** (6-8 hours, day before launch):

**Final Checklist**:
- [ ] All campaigns QA'd and approved
- [ ] All tracking codes in place and tested
- [ ] Landing pages live and conversion tracking verified
- [ ] Email sequences activated and tested
- [ ] Paid campaigns ready to go live
- [ ] Team members assigned (who monitors what?)
- [ ] Escalation process defined (what if something breaks?)
- [ ] Team briefing completed (everyone knows what's happening)

**Launch Timeline** (staggered over 24 hours):
- Hour 0: Email campaigns go live (immediate)
- Hour 2: Paid search campaigns go live (monitor for issues)
- Hour 4: Social ads go live (different audience, stagger helps)
- Hour 8: Outbound sequences begin (don't overwhelm with multiple channels simultaneously from same source)
- Hour 24: Performance review meeting

**Launch Meeting** (30 minutes, 1 hour before first campaigns go live):
- Confirm all systems ready
- Review team assignments (who's monitoring what?)
- Confirm escalation numbers (who to call if issues?)
- Walkthrough launch sequence
- Confirm success metrics (what are we measuring?)

**Live Monitoring** (first 48 hours):

**Hour 0-4: Critical Monitoring**
- Email: Check delivery rate (% successfully delivered)
- Landing pages: Check traffic (are we getting clicks?)
- Form submissions: Check conversion tracking (are forms submitting?)
- Ads: Check approval status (have Google/LinkedIn approved ads?)

**Hour 4-24: Active Monitoring**
- Impressions and clicks flowing?
- Conversion events firing?
- Any bugs or errors?
- Email open rates coming in as expected?

**Day 2-7: Daily Monitoring**
- Daily standup (15 minutes, 9am): What's working? What's not?
- Performance dashboard: Update daily with latest metrics
- Early optimization (if something is clearly underperforming, adjust)

**Performance Monitoring Dashboard**:

**Metrics to Track**:
- Traffic: Total visitors, by source
- Engagement: Email opens, ad clicks, page visits
- Conversion: Leads generated, conversion rate, cost per lead
- Quality: Lead quality, bounce rates
- Revenue (if applicable): Pipeline attributed to campaign

**Daily Performance Review** (by channel):

| Channel | Metric | Target | Actual | Status |
|---------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Email | Open rate | 18-22% | ? | On track / Needs attention |
| Paid Search | CTR | 2-4% | ? | On track / Needs attention |
| LinkedIn | CTR | 1-3% | ? | On track / Needs attention |
| Landing Page | Conversion rate | 5-10% | ? | On track / Needs attention |
| Overall | Cost per lead | $50-100 | ? | On track / Needs attention |

**Troubleshooting During Launch**:

| Issue | Likely Cause | Fix |
|-------|--------------|-----|
| Ads not approved | Policy violation or incomplete information | Check disapproval reason; revise and resubmit |
| No conversions | Tracking not firing or broken landing page | Test form submission; check GA/CRM tracking |
| Very low CTR | Ad copy not compelling or targeting too broad | Review competitors; refine audience targeting |
| High bounce rate | Landing page doesn't match ad promise | Check headline alignment; add more relevant copy |
| Email bouncing | Email list quality or authentication issues | Run list through validation tool; check SPF/DKIM |

**Escalation Process**:
- Issue found → Report to Launch Manager
- Launch Manager → Troubleshoots for 30 minutes
- If not resolved → Escalate to VP Marketing
- VP Marketing → Makes decision (pause campaign, adjust, or continue)

**Launch Documentation**:
- Document what went well (for next campaign)
- Document what went wrong (so we fix it)
- Document what surprised us (learnings)
- Share in post-campaign retrospective

**Deliverables**:
- Launch Coordination Plan (2-3 pages): Timeline, responsibilities, escalation
- Pre-Launch Checklist (1 page): Final verification items
- Performance Monitoring Dashboard (2 pages): Metrics, targets, daily tracking
- Daily Performance Report (1 page, daily): Metrics update
- Troubleshooting Guide (1-2 pages): Common issues and fixes

**Quality Criteria**:
- All campaigns launch within 24-hour window
- Tracking verified for each channel
- Performance is monitored daily
- Issues caught and resolved quickly
- Team is coordinated and clear on roles

## Phase 4 Quality Gate

**Approval Required From**: VP Marketing + Launch Manager

**Review Criteria** (48 hours after launch):
- [ ] All campaigns live and receiving impressions/clicks
- [ ] Tracking data flowing correctly to analytics and CRM
- [ ] Landing page traffic flowing as expected
- [ ] Email sends are delivering successfully (not bouncing)
- [ ] Initial performance baseline established for each channel
- [ ] No major technical issues (forms submitting, links working, etc.)
- [ ] Team monitoring and responding to issues
- [ ] Daily performance dashboard operational
- [ ] First 48-hour report complete (what worked, what didn't)

**Remediation Process** (if gate not passed):
- Tracking not working: Technical troubleshooting and fixes (2-4 hours)
- Ads not approved: Revise and resubmit (1-2 hours)
- Low traffic: Increase budget, check targeting, improve copy (ongoing)
- Email delivery issues: Investigate authentication, list quality (2-4 hours)

**Gate Sign-Off**:
Once approved, you have all campaigns running and can move to Phase 5 (Optimize).

## Common Phase 4 Pitfalls & Solutions

| Pitfall | Prevention | Solution |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Campaigns don't go live on schedule | Create detailed launch timeline | Have backup plan for delays; communicate transparently |
| Tracking doesn't fire correctly | Test tracking before launch | Troubleshoot immediately; don't wait for Phase 5 |
| Landing page conversion rate very low | A/B test variations before full launch | Hot-swap underperforming pages; test new versions quickly |
| Email deliverability issues | Verify SPF/DKIM/DMARC before launch | Get authentication verified immediately |
| Ads not approved due to policy | Review guidelines before launching | Revise copy immediately and resubmit |
| Team gets overwhelmed | Plan for clear monitoring responsibilities | Rotate monitoring duty; take breaks |
| Initial performance below targets | Adjust expectations based on data | Optimize quickly; give campaigns 1-2 weeks before major changes |
